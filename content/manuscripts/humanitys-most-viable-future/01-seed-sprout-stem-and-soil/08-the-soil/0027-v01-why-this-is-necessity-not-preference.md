Here the Soil meets the Seed again, at depth.

If a human being were the isolated, self-contained unit industrial civilization imagined, then coherence would be optional: a preference, a matter of taste. But that is not the kind of creature we are. We are relational organisms whose intelligence, meaning, trust, and sanity are constituted in relationship and degraded by its collapse.

For a creature like that, coherence is not a preference. It is a prerequisite.


The same way oxygen is not a moral position. The same way regulation is not an ideology. They are the conditions under which a certain kind of life remains possible at all. Past a certain threshold, fragmentation is metabolically unsustainable for relational beings. It cannot scale indefinitely, because the organisms it is made of cannot survive it indefinitely.

This is why the book exists: not to argue that coherence would be nice, but to show that, for the kind of beings we actually are, a civilization that keeps fragmenting faster than it can repair is running against its own biology. Living systems can run against their biology only for so long.
