Guilds understood that judgment is not transferred by information alone. Apprentices learned through sustained proximity to people whose hands and choices embodied a craft. Standards protected both practitioner and public, while mutual support gave members a durable social place.

The mentorship door descends from this logic. Providence should preserve the slow transmission of mastery while refusing hereditary privilege, exclusion, and the protection of incumbents from necessary change.
