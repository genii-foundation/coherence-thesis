The third door is the most accessible and scalable. It guides participants through the Seven Initiates over a year.

The curriculum is a constellation rather than the doctrine of one teacher. Mentors contribute a bounded piece of practice, such as an essay, guided experience, recorded teaching, or exercise, situated within one of the seven thresholds. Participants encounter several lineages and methods while moving through the cumulative sequence, then follow the relationships that prove most useful.

This structure also addresses the network's early coordination problem. Mentors gain reach and potential referrals. Participants receive broad development at a lower cost than intensive mentorship. Their experience gives the network more context for later introductions, but only within the consent and data limits described elsewhere.

The curriculum must resist becoming a content funnel that performs depth without relationship. Its purpose is not completion. It is orientation, practice, and a more discerning entrance into the living network.
