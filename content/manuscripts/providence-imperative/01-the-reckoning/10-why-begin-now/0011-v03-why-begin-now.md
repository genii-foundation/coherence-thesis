Trust, practice, and legitimate institutions grow slowly. They cannot be deployed overnight like emergency funds or manufactured equipment. Relationships must form, rules must be tested, and failures must become visible before participants can know whether an institution deserves reliance.

That is the source of urgency. The relevant deadline is not the day a crisis becomes total. Preparation needs time to mature before the need becomes acute. Waiting for universal agreement would consume the margin required to learn.

Fear does not improve coordination. It narrows attention and rewards blunt solutions. The case for beginning now rests instead on the nature of the work: anything built from trust must be grown before it is urgently needed. The architecture, currency, governance, and safeguards that follow are offered as one open experiment in that slower preparation.
