The artifice and Providence both concern human attention, relationship, trust, and the traces a life leaves. The artifice captures those traces to predict and steer behavior. Providence proposes participant-controlled records that help people find and trust one another. Consent changes the moral relationship, but consent alone does not make the systems opposites. Power, error, coercion, access, revocation, security, and governance still decide what the technology becomes.

**Providence is a proposed trust network whose currency is COHERENCE.** The Currency of Presence is the descriptive name for that currency. Bio-Consensus is distinct: it is the proposed primary biometric path by which a unit might be minted. The Membrane would help people find one another. A participant-held record would preserve selected attestations. The Coherence Engine would test whether local signals can support reflection. None of these functions has yet earned the certainty of the earlier paragraph.

Its intended refusals are necessary but not self-proving. It aims to be a protocol rather than a platform, to keep raw signals local, and to reject social scoring. Those aims require a threat model, non-biometric access, data minimization, deletion and export rules, due process, independent audit, and remedies when the system fails. A protocol can still produce landlords. Privacy language becomes architecture only when implementation proves it.

The earlier volumes found a useful image. The extractive machine runs on the current of harvested attention. Providence would turn attention toward trust between people. But the artifice proves only that prediction and capture can work. It does not prove that the same instruments can measure trust, serve consent, or resist capture. Those are the harder tests.
