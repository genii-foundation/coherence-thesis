To those assembling the manor, the question is plain.

Suppose concentration succeeds: platforms, seed, prediction, force, and genomic data remain under the control of a few people and their heirs. That world may still fail its owners. A machine cannot guarantee love, belonging, legitimacy, or a place where others are glad you exist.

Aim capital toward medicine that heals, care that is honored, food and water held as commons, and technology answerable to the people it affects. The appeal rests on material interest, moral responsibility, and the lives of people not yet born, not on a claim that every owner shares one hidden ache.
