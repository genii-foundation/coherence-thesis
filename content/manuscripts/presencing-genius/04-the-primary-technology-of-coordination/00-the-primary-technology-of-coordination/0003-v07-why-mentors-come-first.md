This is why the work begins with mentors rather than an abstract curriculum. A small body of skilled practitioners may help groups listen, disagree, and think together more effectively. Whether that improvement produces better institutions is a testable hypothesis, not a presumed result.

The practice spreads through repetition. A mentor may help one group slow an argument enough to hear its actual disagreement. Members of that group carry the practice into another room, then another. The effect, if it exists, would move through conduct rather than proclamation. That possibility is what the program means to test.
