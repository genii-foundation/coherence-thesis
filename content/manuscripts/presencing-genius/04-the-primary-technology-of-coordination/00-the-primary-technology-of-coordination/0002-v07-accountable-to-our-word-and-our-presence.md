Presence can strengthen accountability by narrowing the distance between what a person says, notices, and does. It cannot guarantee honesty, and a group cannot safely treat felt sincerity as proof. Durable accountability still needs clear commitments, observable conduct, consent, appeal, and correction. Practice may improve those structures. It does not replace them.
