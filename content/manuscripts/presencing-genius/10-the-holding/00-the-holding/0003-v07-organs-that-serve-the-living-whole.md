The second principle is structure in service of life. The text names several possible bodies. B.O.W. Councils would gather experienced practitioners for shared discernment, but practice alone cannot confer unreviewable authority. Membership, powers, conflicts of interest, records, appeal, and public accountability remain to be designed.

The Academy of Muses, Systems Perspective practice, and Circle of Restoration are names for possible creative, systems, and restorative functions. They are not yet operational definitions. Restorative processes in particular need consent, trained facilitation, due process, survivor-centered options, and access to ordinary legal remedies. Community language must never conceal harm or keep a participant from outside help.
