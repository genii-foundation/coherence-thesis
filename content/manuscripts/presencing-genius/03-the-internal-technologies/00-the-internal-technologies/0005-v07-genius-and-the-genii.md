The title reaches toward an older use of *genius*. In Roman religion, a genius could name a tutelary spirit associated with a person or place. The word also belongs to a family concerned with birth and generation. The exact etymology and cultural lineage require verification before publication.

Here the term does not rank one person above another. It names the generative capacity a person already carries and the relational conditions that may help it appear. No mentor gives another person their genius or owns the authority to define it.

The older image keeps some of its life. A grove, hearth, threshold, or person may carry a presence that cannot be reduced to utility. To approach that presence well is to ask what wants to be brought forth without pretending to receive an oracle. The strangeness of *genius* matters because it protects the person from being treated as a bundle of skills waiting for extraction.
