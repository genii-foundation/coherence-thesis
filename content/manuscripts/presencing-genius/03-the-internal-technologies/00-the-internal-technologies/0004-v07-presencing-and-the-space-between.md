Presence is a condition; presencing is an act. To presence something is to help a possibility become actual through relation. What emerges belongs wholly to neither participant. It arises between them.

Many traditions have described relation as more than empty space. This volume uses *the genius of the between* for the insight, possibility, or shared intelligence that may emerge when people attend carefully to one another. The phrase is a philosophical image. It does not establish a separate being or consciousness. We do not manufacture what appears, but we can help make room for it.
