Two principles guide the sketch: sequence and paced depth. Neither permits a mentor to overrule consent. The order should remain open to adaptation for disability, culture, trauma history, prior experience, and individual need. Readiness is discussed with the participant and, where risk is material, reviewed through clear professional standards. Withholding knowledge must never become a tool of mystification or control.

This volume is a threshold and map, not the curriculum itself. Any real course must be piloted cautiously, evaluated independently, and revised from participant experience before its promises can be stated as outcomes.
