*“The fulfillment of our desires, made to serve those around us.”*

Mutual Beneficence asks whether the pursuit of one's desires can also serve others rather than cost them. Self and collective benefit will not always align. The practice requires naming tradeoffs instead of declaring them dissolved.

When alignment is possible, desire becomes contribution rather than sacrifice. When it is not, the conflict should be faced plainly. Beneficence loses meaning when someone else's cost is hidden outside the frame.
