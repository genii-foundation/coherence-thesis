Animistic Awareness invites a person to meet feelings, places, and living systems as presences rather than inert objects. Some traditions understand that claim literally; others receive it as an ethical or poetic stance. This volume does not settle the metaphysics. It asks what greater attention and reciprocity might change.

A feeling may be met rather than immediately suppressed. A river may be approached as a relation with history, limits, and claims upon those who use it. The practice begins in conduct before it reaches metaphysics.
