*“Being more awake and aware than we have ever been, always.”*

Direct Experience is firsthand encounter rather than belief received at second hand. *Living Doctrine* names the effort to test inherited teaching in practice. Experience remains fallible and interpretable. Calling an experience sacred does not place it beyond question.

A map of water is not the taste of water. The distinction matters, but firsthand experience is not pure access to reality. It becomes knowledge through reflection, comparison, and the humility to revise its meaning.
