*“You have to keep breaking your heart until it opens.” Attributed to Rumi; source verification pending.*

Loving Presence joins attention with care. It asks for warmth without sentimentality and steadiness without clinical distance. A person is not the sole author of everything they experience, and loving attention cannot repair every condition. It can change the quality of some encounters.

Sometimes the practice is active help. Sometimes it is the refusal to explain another person's pain before hearing it. Love becomes credible through proportion: neither abandonment nor rescue staged for the rescuer.
