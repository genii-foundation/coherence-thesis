Partial failure means that some parts work while others are captured, ineffective, or harmful. Visible success can hide those losses. Constitutional review should therefore examine every domain, publish adverse findings, and give affected people standing to challenge the institution's own account.

Partial success may still improve real lives. That benefit should be preserved where possible. It should not be used to rename failure elsewhere or silence people carrying its cost.
