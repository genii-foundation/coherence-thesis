The invitation is reciprocal. Any institution acting under these names would owe more than gratitude.

It would owe honesty about current status, results, failures, and changes of plan. People should know whether they are joining research, a pilot, an operating institution, or an aspiration.

It would owe care in material form: safe working conditions, fair compensation, accessible support, and protection from retaliation. Goodwill is not consideration for a person's labor or trust.

It would owe development without coercion. Mentorship and reflection should expand a participant's choices, not turn dependence on the institution into evidence of growth.

It would owe constitutional accountability, practical exit, and repair after harm. Those obligations should be enforceable by people other than the leaders whose conduct is at issue.
