Sidney Tarrow, John McCarthy, Mayer Zald, Frances Fox Piven, Richard Cloward, and Sarah Soule examine movements as they mobilize, institutionalize, or dissolve. Paul DiMaggio and Walter Powell describe coercive, mimetic, and professional pressures that make organizations resemble one another. These lineages offer hypotheses for observation, not a prediction that every change is corruption.
