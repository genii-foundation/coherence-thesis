Growth changes the conditions for which an institution was designed. Providence could adapt usefully or discard constraints that have become inconvenient.

Four pressures deserve attention. Professionalization can import familiar hierarchies. Growth can make compromise feel urgent. Complexity can replace participation with administrative speed. The search for legitimacy can turn external standards into internal rule.

The preceding chapters propose defenses. Their adequacy can be known only through use, independent scrutiny, and comparison with other institutions.
