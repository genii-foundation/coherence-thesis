Complete failure means that the project ends before a durable community or institution exists. Participants may lose income, time, housing stability, relationships, or public standing. Recruitment must state those risks and limit them where possible.

A failed attempt could also make later work harder. It should preserve records, repay obligations, support exit, and publish what was learned. Failure does not become noble merely because the aim was ambitious.

The project owes duties beyond its membership. Neighbors, partners, creditors, and public institutions may bear consequences without having accepted the experiment. Closure planning must include them.
