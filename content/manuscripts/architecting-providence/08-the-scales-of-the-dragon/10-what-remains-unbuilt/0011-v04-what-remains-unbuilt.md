Volume IV moves from design principles toward construction requirements. It proposes a sequence, names economic and governance constraints, outlines technical requirements, describes the human capacities the work would consume, and tests the design through composite scenes.

It has not made the governance architecture operational. It has not built the application, formed the first Scale, validated the Currency of Presence, proved the safeguards, or tested the population ranges. The Cardinal Scale in these pages is a design fiction. Its events do not establish implementation.

The manuscript's strongest contribution is an inventory of what a serious attempt would have to confront: founding power, compatible capital, consent, privacy, exit, stewardship, culture, conflict, ecological limits, and the adaptive pressure of success. Several of those obligations remain unresolved, and some require legal, technical, empirical, or authorial decisions beyond a literary pass.

Volume V turns from institutional architecture toward human purpose and the people who might build. It should not be presented as an implementation report that the published work does not contain.

This volume ends with proposals in hand, evidence still owed, and the invitation to test the difference.

*The question is still a choice.*
