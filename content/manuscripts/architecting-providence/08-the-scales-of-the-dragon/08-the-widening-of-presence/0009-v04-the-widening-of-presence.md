Relational practice does not automatically produce ecological responsibility. People can be attentive to one another and still damage a watershed. The connection must be made through knowledge, limits, governance, and repeated contact with place.

Yet presence can widen attention. The practice of noticing another person may help a community notice soil, water, weather, and the consequences of its own use. That possibility needs material tests: ecological baselines, public measures, adverse-outcome reporting, and authority for the land's limits to change human plans.

To say the Earth awakens to itself is therefore poetic. It names the hope that human perception may become answerable to the living systems it affects. The metaphor does not excuse the accounting.
