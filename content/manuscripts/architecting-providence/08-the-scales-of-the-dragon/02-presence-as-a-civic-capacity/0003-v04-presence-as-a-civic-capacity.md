Presence is not only a private or spiritual concern. It can be civic practice. Communities can make time for repair, train facilitators, protect deliberation from manufactured urgency, and support people whose role is to hold difficult processes. These are concrete investments in the conditions under which collective judgment may improve.

The economic metaphor has limits. Attention is not labor, regulation is not virtue, and trust is not a commodity detached from context. Calling coherence a form of capital should not authorize anyone to accumulate power over others. The point is narrower: institutions can resource the capacities they currently consume without tending.
