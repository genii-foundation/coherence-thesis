*A design meets evidence only in a particular place, with real people and real stakes.*

A design remains an abstraction until people test it in a particular place under real constraints. This volume calls the proposed first embodiment the Cardinal Scale. The eighty-seven-person watershed described here is a composite used to test the architecture in thought. Its people, history, software, donor, and outcomes are not evidence that the community exists.

The terminology matters. A single community is a Scale. ICONS, *Individual Communities Observing Natural Sovereignty*, names the plural network of Scales. Each Scale would govern itself in a particular culture and ecology while using shared protocols to coordinate with others.

This proposal resembles some accounts of a network state, but it should not inherit claims of sovereignty without legal and democratic scrutiny. A Scale would still operate within existing jurisdictions, affect neighbors and ecosystems, and owe duties to people who never consented to its internal constitution.

The numbers describe two levels. Eighty-seven is the imagined founding cohort. Roughly one hundred and fifty is a provisional upper range for a direct-trust group. A bioregional Scale approaching five hundred would therefore require nested groups and accountable interfaces among them. The manuscript does not establish that five hundred people can remain a single field of direct relational coherence.
