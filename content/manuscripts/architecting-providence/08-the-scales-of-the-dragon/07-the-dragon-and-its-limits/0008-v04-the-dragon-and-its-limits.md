The dragon is offered as an image, not a claim of common doctrine across cultures. Imagine the Earth as a body and the Scales as distinct plates along its surface, each catching different light. Providence is not the dragon. It is the connective tissue through which communities might recognize one another.

The metaphor earns its place only while it remains bounded. Ecosystems are interdependent, but cells are not citizens and organisms are not constitutions. The image asks what coordination could feel like if belonging to a whole did not require erasing the part.
