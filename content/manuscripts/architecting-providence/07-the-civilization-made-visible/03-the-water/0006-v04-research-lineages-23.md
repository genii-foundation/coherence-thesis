Robert Yin's work on case study research examines complex systems in context. This chapter uses fiction for a narrower purpose: to place several proposed mechanisms in one scene and expose their interaction. It does not acquire empirical force from the case study tradition.

Roger Fisher and William Ury's Getting to Yes (1981) distinguishes positions from interests. Howard Zehr's Changing Lenses (1990) and community restorative practice examine processes aimed at repairing relationship. The manuscript should not claim that such processes always outlast adversarial ones without specifying context and evidence.

Ostrom's work on accessible conflict processes and graduated responses informs the imagined reversion threshold. The scene should still be judged as a design proposal, not a demonstration of her findings.
