Mahlinah has held relational stewardship for two years. She facilitates governance, supports conflict work, and helps maintain constitutional accountability. She is good at the role, and some people think rotation would discard competence for the sake of a rule.

The rule exists because competence can create dependency. Mahlinah has not failed. Her authority ends on schedule so that excellence does not become a permanent claim to office.

She moves into a bounded mentorship role with no veto over her successor. On Tuesday she meets Elan, the incoming steward, for the third time. The meeting is awkward because Elan is still learning and both of them know it. The community accepts a temporary loss of fluency in exchange for succession that is real.
