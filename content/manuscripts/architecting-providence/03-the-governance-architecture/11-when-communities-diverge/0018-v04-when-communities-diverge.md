*A constitution that cannot accommodate genuine disagreement is not a constitution. It is a loyalty oath.*

Providence is designed for communities that will differ in law, culture, ecology, and ordinary practice. Some differences will be local adaptations. Others will expose competing interpretations or values that deliberation does not resolve.

Coherence does not require identical choices. It does require a way to decide when local variation breaches a shared commitment. That process must guard against both central conformity and the use of local autonomy to excuse harm.
