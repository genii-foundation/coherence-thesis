Stewardship is learned through bounded responsibility and accountability, not conferred by a credential or personality judgment. Providence proposes three fields of practice rather than a universal rank.

Participatory stewardship holds a defined part of community or project life. The role needs a clear mandate, supervision, feedback from people affected, and an end date.

Relational stewardship supports deliberation, conflict repair, and the people carrying operational duties. Greater authority must bring stronger disclosure, review, and appeal.

Constitutional stewardship carries principles and institutional memory through succession. It may advise on change, but it should not become a permanent caste or a veto held by founders.
