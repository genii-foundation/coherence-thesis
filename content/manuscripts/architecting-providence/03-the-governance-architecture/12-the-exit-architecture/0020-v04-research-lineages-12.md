Albert Hirschman's *Exit, Voice, and Loyalty* (1970) shows how the availability of exit shapes the quality of voice. People who cannot leave bargain under constraint. Allen Buchanan's work on secession and self-determination asks when persistent denial of voice can justify political separation. Providence needs its own rules rather than a direct analogy to firms or states.
