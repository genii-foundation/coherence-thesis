Relational familiarity weakens as a network grows. Records and formal review may carry some of the missing context, but they also simplify it. Providence does not yet know whether its mechanisms can preserve meaningful constitutional accountability across a large network.

The purpose of divergence governance is modest and difficult: communities should remain distinct while staying answerable for the commitments they share. That is coherence without conformity.
