Exit is a constitutional requirement: participation must remain voluntary enough that communities and participants can leave. This chapter makes that requirement operational: what leaving actually looks like, what participants and communities take with them when they leave, what obligations they retain, and what claims they can make on shared infrastructure they helped build.

Exit must be practical rather than ceremonial. The cost rises when participants would lose identity, contribution history, relationships, or essential infrastructure. A credible process therefore needs portability, notice, assistance, and protection against retaliation.

Exit also raises unresolved record questions. A participant may wish to carry a private archive while revoking public attestations. Other people may retain records created through shared transactions. The architecture must distinguish a participation record, personal archive, credential, wallet, and third-party attestation, then define export, deletion, expiration, revocation, and recovery for each.
