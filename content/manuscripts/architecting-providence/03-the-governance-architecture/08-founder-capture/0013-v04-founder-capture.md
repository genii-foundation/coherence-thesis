Founders begin with unusual knowledge, relationships, visibility, and informal authority. Those advantages can harden into control without an explicit power grab.

Responses include term limits, early succession planning, non-founder authority in consequential decisions, public conflicts-of-interest records, and a culture that treats the founding vision as common property.

Founder restraint is not a safeguard. The legal and governance design must distribute authority, require succession, expose exceptions, and provide remedies when founders violate the rules. Whether any arrangement can make capture impossible is a claim for evidence, not declaration.
