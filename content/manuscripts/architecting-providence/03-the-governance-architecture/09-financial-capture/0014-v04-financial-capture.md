Financial capture often works without an explicit bargain. Leaders learn which language, programs, and decisions preserve support. Repeated accommodation can redirect the mission without one decisive act of betrayal.

Responses include diversified funding, published concentration limits, governance firewalls, restrictions on conditioned gifts, and audits that compare program decisions with funder preferences. Any numeric limit needs modeling and approval before it becomes constitutional text.
