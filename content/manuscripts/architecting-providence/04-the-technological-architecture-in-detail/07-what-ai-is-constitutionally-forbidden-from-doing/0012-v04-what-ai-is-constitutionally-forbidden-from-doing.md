The prohibitions concern authority, data, and contestability.

AI cannot determine trustworthiness. It may organize attestations, but a score or recommendation must not become a technical oracle with practical authority over a person.

AI cannot make binding governance decisions. It may support procedure and analysis, but participants must understand, contest, and decide the outcome.

AI cannot operate on participant data without specific and ongoing consent. Enrollment consent is not enough for continuous or newly purposed processing. Participants need clear notice, withdrawal without loss of basic access, and an account of what deletion can and cannot undo.

AI cannot be deployed when consequential effects are opaque or unchallengeable. Audits must be proportionate to risk and available to independent reviewers. Participants need reasons they can understand, a human appeal, and a remedy when the system causes harm.
