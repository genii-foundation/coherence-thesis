Stuart Russell's *Human Compatible* (2019) introduces the problem of systems pursuing formal objectives that diverge from human intent. Frank Pasquale, Virginia Eubanks, Safiya Umoja Noble, and Kate Crawford examine opacity, automated inequality, and the material power behind algorithmic systems.

The Algorithmic Justice League, AI Now Institute, and Partnership on AI publish accountability frameworks. Because capabilities, vendors, law, and standards change quickly, Providence needs a dated technical and legal review rather than a permanent list of approved methods.
