AI may assist with work that remains intelligible, voluntary, and reversible. The people affected must retain authority over meaning, ethics, and governance.

Sensemaking assistance: a system may summarize documents and surface patterns. It must preserve provenance, uncertainty, dissenting records, and the possibility that the pattern is spurious.

Translation across difference: a system may assist communication across languages and vocabularies. Participants need access to the original, qualified translators for consequential matters, and a way to challenge distortion.

Resource and relationship matching: a system may suggest connections under participant-controlled preferences. It must not infer sensitive traits or hide why one person was surfaced and another was not.

Governance support: a system may track proposals, deadlines, procedures, and public records. It may not decide the substance or silently narrow what enters deliberation.
