The first version should not ship unless the following prohibitions are enforceable, observable, and accompanied by remedies.

It must not optimize for engagement. Success measures should concern completed purposes, comprehension, consent, and harm, not time spent or repeated return.

It must not sell advertising or disclose participant data to advertisers. The revenue model must remain viable without behavioral targeting.

The operator must not own participant profiles. The data model should enforce consent, purpose limitation, portability, deletion, and an honest account of records that cannot be withdrawn from other parties.

AI must not make consequential decisions about participants. Each assisted function needs disclosure, a comprehensible reason, a human appeal path, and a way to refuse without losing basic access.

The operator must not be able to sell or repurpose the commons infrastructure unilaterally. This is still a requirement, not a legal or technical fact. Ownership, licensing, protocol governance, operator succession, and remedies need public documentation before the claim can be tested.
