Langdon Winner's "Do Artifacts Have Politics?" (1980) and Value Sensitive Design, developed by Batya Friedman and Peter Kahn, ask how technical choices embody social values. They support requirements that can be inspected in architecture, interfaces, and governance rather than left in a mission statement.

Cory Doctorow and Yochai Benkler examine interoperability and platform lock-in. Their work supports portability and replaceable services, though no technical pattern can make an institution legally non-acquirable by itself.

B.J. Fogg's work on persuasive technology, Tristan Harris's writing on attention capture, and research on digital wellbeing examine how interface choices shape behavior. They support scrutiny of engagement optimization, though the manuscript still needs a dated evidence review for its specific design claims.
