The first version would provide four bounded functions.

Identity and participation management: participants would manage identity, visibility, consent, and exit. The design requirement is participant control. Key recovery, revocation, third-party copies, and the lifecycle of exported records remain unresolved.

Community discovery and coordination: participants and communities would find one another through preferences they control, without engagement optimization. Tools should support deliberation, consent, conflict repair, and stewardship without imposing one governance model.

Trust attestation and communication: participants would create and disclose specific, consensual attestations. Such attestations should not become a universal score, a hidden rank, or a substitute for equal political standing.

Commons contribution and access: participants would contribute to and use a shared record of governance experiments, ecological practice, conflict repair, and community development. Attribution, licensing, privacy, moderation, and stewardship would need explicit rules.
