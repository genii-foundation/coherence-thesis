Measurement determines which effects become visible to a decision process. It also creates proxies that can be mistaken for the thing itself. GDP and engagement metrics illustrate the problem: useful within a defined purpose, dangerous when treated as complete accounts of value.

Any measure must remain connected to the value it claims to represent. Once a proxy becomes a target, people can optimize the proxy while degrading the underlying good. The answer is not measurement everywhere. It is bounded measurement, plural evidence, public limits, and the ability to stop using a measure when it distorts behavior.

The proposed measures include participant accounts of relational health, evidence of developmental progress, ecological conditions in the places communities inhabit, and audits of constitutional practice. None should be treated as a complete proxy for what it observes. Each needs a method, a defined use, an appeal process, and an account of who could be harmed when the measure is wrong.

Proof of Council is one proposed ancillary path for recognizing contribution. In that design, a council process might attest that people gathered and deliberated under agreed conditions. Bio-Consensus is the proposed local biometric reading of presence, and COHERENCE is the proposed unit of the Currency of Presence. Proof of Council would remain secondary to Bio-Consensus, and it should not mint COHERENCE unless the procedure, validator, consent, evidence, appeal, and revocation rules have been tested.

Each measure requires human judgment. That judgment introduces bias and power as well as context. Automation cannot remove the responsibility; neither can the phrase human judgment.
