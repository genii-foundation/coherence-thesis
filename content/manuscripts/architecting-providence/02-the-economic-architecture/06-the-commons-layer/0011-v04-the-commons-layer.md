*The commons is not what is left over after ownership has been established. It is what must be protected from ownership if the conditions of collective life are to remain viable.*

Within the proposed commons layer, shared standards, collective learning, and ecological stewardship require common governance. Personal data and contextual trust claims remain subject to participant rights rather than common ownership. The operational questions are ownership, access, contribution, moderation, governance, and protection from enclosure.

Infrastructure controlled by one actor remains a service, however benevolent the operator. Its terms, fees, and access can change. Common infrastructure therefore needs a legal owner, governance process, maintenance budget, fork path, and public remedy when stewards fail.

Elinor Ostrom's work on governed commons offers a starting point. Digital identity, personal data, and trust attestations also create privacy and rights questions that resource commons do not answer by analogy alone.
