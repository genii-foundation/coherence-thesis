The first community may rely on participant commitments and philanthropy. Later infrastructure may demand more capital than those sources can provide. Providence needs transition criteria before scarcity makes the decision for it: permitted instruments, concentration limits, review authority, refusal conditions, and a plan for losing a major funder.

Patience about scale protects the substance of the work. Growth is useful only while the safeguards grow with it.
