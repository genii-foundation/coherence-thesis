Yochai Benkler's work on commons-based peer production examines large projects sustained by motives beyond direct payment. It does not show that monetary incentives are unnecessary in every setting. David Graeber's work on debt and value widens the field beyond market exchange by asking how obligations arise within social relationships.

Social enterprise law asks how institutions can bind themselves to social and environmental purposes. Relevant sources include B Corporation standards, benefit corporation statutes, and Antony Page and Robert Katz's work on benefit corporation law. Certification and legal form should not be confused, and neither guarantees constitutional fidelity.
