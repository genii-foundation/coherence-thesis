Endowments can sustain long missions, but donors and investment managers retain influence. Cooperatives can align ownership with participation, but membership rules and capital accounts still distribute power. Public funding can reach long horizons, but law and political change shape its terms. Earned revenue can support independence while pulling the institution toward the customers most able to pay.

The record does not identify one clean source of capital. It suggests a portfolio with limits on concentration, transparent conditions, and an exit plan for each dependency. Whether venture capital can fit any bounded component of Providence remains a governance question, not a moral verdict supplied by the category.
