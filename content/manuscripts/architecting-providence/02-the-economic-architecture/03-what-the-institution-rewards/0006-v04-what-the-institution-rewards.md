*Every currency teaches its holders what to become.*

*Adapted from The Coherence Thesis, Volume III*

*What an institution rewards is what it values, regardless of what it says it values. The internal economy is the institution's real constitution.*

An institution's internal economy is the way it distributes money, recognition, access, and opportunity. Those rewards can contradict its stated values while its constitution remains untouched. Repeated incentives usually govern behavior more effectively than ceremonial language.

Providence must therefore test its economic rules against its principles. If it rewards extraction while praising regeneration, the reward is the operative policy.
