*We are not short of knowledge, or of will, or of people who care. We are short of the means to act on them together, and that absence is the shape of every crisis in this book.*

*Adapted from The Coherence Thesis, Volume III*

*A map is not a building. But a building begun without a map is not architecture. It is improvisation at scale, and improvisation at scale has many ways to falter.*

Volume III described a design space. This chapter inventories the questions that still stand between that design and construction. It does not claim that the earlier volume used an apparatus or opening disclaimer absent from its current text.

Construction creates pressures that design can postpone. Builders simplify, choose among incomplete options, and compromise toward what can be done now. Those pressures are unavoidable. They should be visible.
