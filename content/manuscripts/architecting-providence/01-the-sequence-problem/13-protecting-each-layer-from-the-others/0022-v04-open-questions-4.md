A long horizon can easily become ceremonial. Public measures and regular review can at least make erosion visible, though no design guarantees that later participants will care.

Another open question concerns amendment. Entrenchment can protect future obligations, but it can also preserve a mistake. Stability and adaptability remain a permanent governance tension.

*The long clock matters only if people can live inside it. Food must be grown, children taught, elders held, and the labor sustained without selling the conditions that made the community worth joining.*
