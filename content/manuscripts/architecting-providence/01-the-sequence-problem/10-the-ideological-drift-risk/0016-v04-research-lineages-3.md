Robert Michels' account of oligarchy asks why growing organizations concentrate control despite democratic commitments. Richard Cornforth's work on cooperative governance examines practices that may resist that drift. Sidney Tarrow, Frances Fox Piven, and Richard Cloward describe the tension between a movement's disruptive force and the organization required to sustain it.

Yochai Benkler offers a different line of inquiry: whether network structures can support cooperation through transparency, local autonomy, shared protocols, and graduated responses to breaches. These traditions frame the problem. None proves that Providence's proposed nesting will solve it.
