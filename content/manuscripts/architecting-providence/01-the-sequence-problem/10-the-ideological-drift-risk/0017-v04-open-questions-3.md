The pace of growth cannot be set in advance. It should depend on evidence from the first community, the stability of its safeguards, and the cost of delay.

The boundary between local and network authority also remains unsettled. Subsidiarity favors local decisions when local action is adequate. Providence still needs a process for deciding when ecological effects, rights, shared infrastructure, or cross-community disputes require wider authority.
