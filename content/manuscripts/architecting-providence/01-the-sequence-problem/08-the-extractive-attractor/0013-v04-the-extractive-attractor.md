The extractive attractor is the pressure on a growing institution to adopt the measures its environment rewards. Revenue, user acquisition, growth, and engagement can displace a purpose they fail to measure.

The pressure does not require bad intent. Capital providers, accounting systems, employment practices, and planning tools all carry assumptions about success. A nonextractive institution may absorb those assumptions simply to become legible to the systems around it.

Culture alone cannot hold that pressure. Legal form, ownership, governance, funding, and data practice must reinforce the commitment. Constitutional protection can increase resistance, though it cannot make capture impossible.
