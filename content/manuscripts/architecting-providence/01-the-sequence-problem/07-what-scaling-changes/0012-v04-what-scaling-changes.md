*What scales is never exactly what worked. The question is whether what is lost in the scaling is recoverable, and whether what is preserved is sufficient.*

What works in a small group changes as participation grows. Direct acquaintance gives way to procedure. Informal memory gives way to records. Roles that once rotated through friendship acquire institutional weight. Scaling is therefore a change in kind, not simply an increase in quantity.

Providence would face the same pressure. Its task is not to prevent change, but to distinguish adaptation from abandonment of the founding constraints.
