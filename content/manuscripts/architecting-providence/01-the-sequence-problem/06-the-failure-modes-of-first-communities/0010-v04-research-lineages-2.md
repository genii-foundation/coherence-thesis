Several traditions illuminate the first community without supplying a template. The Benedictine Rule and Adalbert de Vogüé's commentary raise questions about founding authority and succession. Cooperative histories contrast New Harmony with the Rochdale Pioneers, while Johnston Birchall examines the principles that helped cooperatives persist. The Intentional Communities Directory and *Communities* magazine collect accounts of communities that survived or dissolved. These sources need direct verification before their lessons are treated as settled.

Jon Elster's *Ulysses Unbound* (2000) and Bruce Ackerman's *We the People* series examine how founding decisions constrain later choices. Their central warning applies here: early commitments may protect a community from capture, or merely preserve the founders' distribution of power.
