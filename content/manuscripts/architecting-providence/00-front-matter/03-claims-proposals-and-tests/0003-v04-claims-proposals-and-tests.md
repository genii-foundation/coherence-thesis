Design questions have several kinds of answer. Some can be resolved through present research. Some require a provisional decision before enough evidence exists. Others can only be answered by people living with the consequences. The text should make those states legible rather than giving every proposal the same tone of certainty.

The research passages name useful intellectual lineages, but they do not convert those lineages into proof. The scenarios test the architecture in imagination. They are not reports from an existing Providence community. The open questions remain open until evidence or authorial decision closes them.

The governing posture is patient, practical, and corrigible. The nest must eventually bear weight, but it should not be declared load-bearing before anyone has stood in it.
