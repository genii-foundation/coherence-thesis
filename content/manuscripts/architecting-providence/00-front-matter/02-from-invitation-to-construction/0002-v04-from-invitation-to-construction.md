Volume III closed with an invitation. This volume opens by taking it seriously.

The distance from a map to a commitment is not crossed by argument alone. Volume III described constitutional, technical, economic, and human requirements for Providence. It did not establish that the design would work in practice. Volume IV turns those requirements into construction questions.

One question governs the volume: how might Providence be built without becoming what it was designed to replace? Movements against extraction can become extractive. Communities organized against capture can concentrate power. Decentralized systems can quietly acquire a center. Good intent does not prevent these outcomes. Structure can make them easier or harder.

Readers can enter here without Volume III, but this volume assumes its core distinctions. It will cite them when they matter and will not invent sections or promises that the current Volume III does not contain.
