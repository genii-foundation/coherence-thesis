Robert Kegan's work on adult development and *Immunity to Change*, written with Lisa Laskow Lahey, examines how existing commitments can obstruct desired change. Providence may draw questions from that work without adopting its stages as a measure of worth or eligibility.

Jack Mezirow and Mary Belenky with colleagues examine transformative learning and ways of knowing. Their work may inform learning environments without proving a universal developmental sequence.

Arnold van Gennep and Victor Turner examine how communities mark separation, transition, and incorporation. Their descriptions do not justify manufacturing ordeals or borrowing rites from cultures to which a community is not accountable.
