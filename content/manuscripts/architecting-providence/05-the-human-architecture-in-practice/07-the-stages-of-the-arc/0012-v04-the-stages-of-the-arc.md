Encounter: a person meets the work through a text, application, gathering, or invitation. The institution owes clear terms, current status, costs, and a path to decline.

Engagement: a participant joins shared work and governance. They need orientation, accessible support, and protection from hazing disguised as developmental friction.

Contribution: a participant accepts bounded responsibility for work, governance, or support. The role needs authority, compensation, supervision, and an end.

Stewardship: a participant holds bounded constitutional responsibility. Rotation, appeal, conflicts-of-interest rules, and care for the steward remain mandatory.

Legacy: a participant transmits memory or mentorship without retaining permanent authority. Honor should not become a veto.
