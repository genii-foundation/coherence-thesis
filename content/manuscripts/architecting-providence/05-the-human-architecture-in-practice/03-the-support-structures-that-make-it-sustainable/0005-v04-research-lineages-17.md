James MacGregor Burns, Howard Gardner, and scholars of social entrepreneurship examine leadership and institution building. Their work may illuminate founding capacity, but it should not be used to turn personality traits into an exclusionary founder type.

Christina Maslach's burnout research identifies workload, control, reward, community, fairness, and values alignment as important domains of mismatch between people and organizations. Those domains offer a practical review of founding roles.

Arlie Hochschild's work on emotional labor, Joan Tronto's ethics of care, and feminist scholarship on undervalued care work provide a sharper test. Institutions that praise care externally may still underpay and exhaust the people who perform it internally.
