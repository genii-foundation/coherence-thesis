This book is written without scholarly apparatus. There are no footnotes. Citations to specific projects, researchers, and traditions appear in prose rather than in references. The intellectual lineages in which the book stands are not exhaustively credited in the academic sense. This is a deliberate choice worth explaining.

The book’s argument is structural and cumulative. It asks readers to hold a sustained line of thought across thirteen chapters, from physiological regulation and relational coherence through extraction, coordination infrastructure, economics, governance, AI, and the human experience of civilizational transition. The argument depends on readers following that line without being pulled out of it. Footnotes, parenthetical citations, and academic apparatus interrupt the reading the book requires. They serve a different purpose, verifying sources for readers who want to evaluate underlying scholarship, and are essential in academic contexts. They are not what this book is.

The book is written for builders: software engineers, designers, governance practitioners, contemplatives, ecologists, funders, organizers, and the wider community of people who can feel the necessity of the work it describes. These readers do not primarily need citations verified. They need the argument articulated clearly enough to evaluate it on its own terms and decide whether to engage with what comes next. The form serves the function.

This does not mean the intellectual work is unmoored. The book makes specific claims about specific projects: Sardex in Sardinia, Mondragón in the Basque Country, Citizens’ Assemblies in Ireland and France, vTaiwan, Constitutional AI, the McKinsey survey on regenerative agriculture, OpenCivics, the Inner Development Goals framework, the Capital Institute’s Regenerative Communities Network, the IDG Summit, the 2025 NCEAS biodiversity monitoring assessment, and COP16 programming on traditional ecological knowledge. Each is a real organization, project, or piece of research that interested readers can verify directly. The book names them so verification remains possible without apparatus that would interrupt the reading.
