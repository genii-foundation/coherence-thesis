The claim beneath this chapter, separate from any specific project, is that the people who would build coherent civilization are already here. They are scattered across many traditions, working in many forms, mostly invisible to each other and to mainstream institutional discourse, mostly operating in conditions of severe undercapitalization relative to the urgency. The builders exist. Whether they will find each other before fragmentation becomes irreversible is the open question.

If the regenerative-coordination world and the inner-development world remain in their respective silos, the work of each will continue to be limited by what the other already knows. If the contemplative technologists, the bioregional coordinators, and the coordination-infrastructure builders remain unable to find each other, the integration the present moment requires will not occur. If the AI alignment research community continues to operate in isolation from the relational, governance, and economic infrastructure work that determines whether alignment can be achieved at the civilizational level, the technical work will fail to translate into the civilizational outcomes its participants are hoping for.

The work is real. The builders are real. What is missing is the connective tissue.
