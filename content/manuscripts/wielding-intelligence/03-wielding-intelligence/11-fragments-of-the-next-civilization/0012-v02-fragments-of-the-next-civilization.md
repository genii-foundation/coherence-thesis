What comes next is not visible because it has not been built. Its possible builders are alive now, and some have begun working in fragments, in many places and at small scales, mostly outside dominant institutional discourse. Whether those fragments coalesce into something coherent enough to constitute the next civilization is the question of the next several decades.

The outcome is genuinely undetermined. Civilizations have crossed thresholds like this before, sometimes successfully and sometimes not. Some factors that distinguish successful crossings cannot be controlled, while others can. What cannot be controlled is whether climate, technology, and geopolitical pressure remain within a metabolizable range during the transition. What can be controlled, in part, is whether people who could build coherent alternatives find one another in time, develop the inner capacities the work requires, and build relational, economic, and governance infrastructure that lets the work proceed at the scale and speed the moment asks for.

This is the work the book has described and invites readers into. The conditions are not waiting. The infrastructure is not being built fast enough. Builders are not finding one another at the density the urgency requires.

This chapter asks readers to recognize the threshold they inhabit, grieve what is being lost, feel what is happening in their bodies and relationships, and from that more accurate perception begin the work that is theirs to do. Not work the book prescribes, but work each reader can recognize once the conditions are clear.

*The book is almost finished. What remains is to synthesize its account of wielding intelligence and make as direct an invitation as conditions allow.*
