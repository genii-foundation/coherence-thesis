Despite their surface differences, people working in these adjacent design spaces share several characteristics worth naming.

They tend to work in conditions that are structurally undercapitalized relative to other civilizational infrastructure. In any given year, capital flowing into AI capability development exceeds, by orders of magnitude, the total flowing into the coordination, contemplative, governance, and regenerative-economics work the book has described. Builders in this field have generally learned to do more with less, refuse scale that would compromise integrity, hold long time horizons, and work where the work matters more than visibility.

They tend to have spent serious time on at least one form of personal practice, somatic, contemplative, therapeutic, or relational, that has changed their understanding of what coordination requires. This is not incidental. The chapters on physiology and relational coherence argued that institutional architectures cannot be more coherent than the nervous systems that constitute them. Builders most likely to succeed at coherent coordination infrastructure have done enough regulatory work of their own to recognize what coherence feels like in the body. This is a quiet selector, but it operates.

They tend to be skeptical of the founder-as-permanent-authority pattern that has captured most platform-scale technology projects. Many have lived through cycles in which capital, ideology, or the gravitational pull of success captured mission-driven organizations. The builders most worth working with have developed an immune response to those patterns and are willing to design against them even when that slows the work.

They tend to work across more than one domain. The most useful builders in this field are rarely pure specialists. They have worked in technology and contemplative practice, finance and ecology, governance and trauma research, academia and grassroots organizing. This cross-domain experience is a kind of substrate. It permits the integration work the previous interstitial named, the meeting of worlds the technology question requires.
