The chapter’s deepest claim, separate from any specific project, is that the people who would build coherent civilization are already here. They are scattered across many traditions, working in many forms, mostly invisible to one another, mostly invisible to mainstream institutional discourse, mostly operating in conditions of severe undercapitalization relative to the urgency. The question is not whether the builders exist. The question is whether they will find one another before fragmentation becomes irreversible.

The next phase depends on this question. If the regenerative-coordination and inner-development worlds remain in their silos, each will remain limited by what the other knows. If contemplative technologists, bioregional coordinators, and coordination-infrastructure builders cannot find one another, the integration this moment requires will not occur. If AI-alignment research remains isolated from the relational, governance, and economic infrastructure that determines whether alignment can be achieved at a civilizational level, technical work will not translate into the outcomes its participants hope for.

The work is real. The builders are real. What is missing is the connective tissue.

*The next chapter turns to what humanity is moving through now: the developmental and psychological dimensions of the threshold, its grief, disorientation, and possibility, and what it asks of people living through it. Who builds, and how, is inseparable from what it feels like to be alive in a civilization crossing conditions it has never crossed before.*
