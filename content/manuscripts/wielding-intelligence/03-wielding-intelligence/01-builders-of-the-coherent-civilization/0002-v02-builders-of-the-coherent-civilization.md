*We are, all of us, already writing it: in every system we build, every nervous system we steady or fray, every bit of trust we extend or betray. The only question is whether we do it knowingly.*

— The Coherence Thesis, Vol. I

Ten chapters have argued that coherent coordination infrastructure has to be built, and that the design space for building it has been radically underdeveloped relative to what the present moment requires. This chapter turns to the people doing the work: not a list of allies, not a directory readers should join, but a report on the state of a field whose participants do not yet know they are a field.

There is more happening than is visible from any single vantage. There is also less than the urgency requires. Both are true at once.
