*We are, all of us, already writing it: in every system we build, every nervous system we steady or fray, every bit of trust we extend or betray. The only question is whether we do it knowingly.*

*The Coherence Thesis, Vol. I*

The book has spent ten chapters arguing that coherent coordination infrastructure must be built and that the design space for it remains radically underdeveloped for this moment. This chapter turns to the people doing the work. Not as a list of allies the book is endorsing. Not as a directory readers should join. As an honest report on a field whose participants do not yet recognize themselves as a field.

There is more happening than is visible from any single vantage. There is also less than the urgency requires. Both are true at once, and the chapter has to hold both.
