The same discipline that governed the previous chapters applies here. Providence is not a builder collective. It does not represent or speak for people working in the worlds named above. It does not claim alignment with projects it has not been in direct relationship with. The projects named in this chapter, OpenCivics, the Capital Institute, ReFi DAO, the Inner Development Goals, the Mind & Life Institute, the contemplative-sustainability researchers, the AI alignment community, and the Indigenous-led conservation initiatives, do their own work on their own terms. The book mentions them descriptively, not as affiliations.

At its early stage, Providence is being designed to operate at the layer Chapter Five described: to help aligned actors find one another through the matching architecture, support small-group meetings, and develop over time the trust infrastructure and coordination substrate that more ambitious coordination would require. The bet is that people working in these adjacent worlds hold enormous latent coordination capacity, and that helping them find one another at greater density than current architectures permit would produce value the current institutional landscape cannot generate.

This is the modest claim. Providence is not the field. It is one attempt to build coordination infrastructure adequate to it. The field exists with or without Providence and will continue to develop whether this project succeeds. Providence is trying to contribute relational infrastructure through which the field can begin to find itself.
