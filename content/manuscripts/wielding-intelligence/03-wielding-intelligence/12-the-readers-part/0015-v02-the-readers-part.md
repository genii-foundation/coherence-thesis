Readers who have made it this far may agree, resist, remain uncertain, or carry only parts of the argument forward. The book does not ask for agreement as proof of attention. It offers an account of present conditions that readers can test against their own experience and judgment.

If the book has done its work, it has given readers language they can use or reject. It has named one account of the destabilization beneath daily life, traced fragmentation across institutional, ecological, relational, and informational dimensions, and proposed forms of work that such conditions may require. Readers must decide what, if anything, the account clarifies.

What comes next is not the book’s to decide. It is the reader’s.

The Final Note after the next chapter makes a specific invitation as direct as the book can make it. First, the closing must do one more thing. The book names Providence and positions itself partly as the project’s founding document. Throughout, it has maintained a discipline of honesty about what the project is, what it is not, and what it has not yet figured out. The next chapter carries that discipline to its conclusion. It is the book’s most direct accounting of its own choices.
