This chapter has to do something different.

The previous eleven chapters have made an analytical, structural, and constructive argument. They have described coherence, the effects of extraction, infrastructure that could begin to rebuild it, intelligence in right relationship with life, and the people doing the work. By this point, the argument has been made.

*To describe an institution only in the language of its architecture is to describe a house only by its blueprints. One must also say what it is to wake within its walls.* The book has not yet sat with the experience of being alive at this threshold: the destabilization people feel, the grief that arrives as inherited institutions lose legitimacy in real time, and the strange texture of a period when what comes next is not visible and what is ending has not finished ending. This chapter acknowledges that experience because its argument cannot land for readers trying to manage a civilizational disorientation for which most have not been given language.

The argument cannot land because a nervous system cannot integrate it while still processing the conditions it addresses.
