# Voice and Canon: What Must Survive Editing

The fix for inflation is specificity, not deflation. A de-slopped Coherence Thesis should sound more like Tao Yu, not less. This file defines the target register, the protected inventory, and calibration rewrites. When in doubt between a safe flat sentence and the author's stranger, more concrete one, keep the author's.

## The target register

The corpus at its best does five things. Push every rewrite toward these shapes.

**One abstraction anchored to one concrete, testable image.**
> "Trust is the substrate of coordination" (anchored two sentences later to leverage rising as trust falls)
> "vagal tone is audible"
> "Money, at its root, is memory"

**Named people doing specific things.**
> "gets the diabetic grandfather to the table."
> "She thinks about it, and answers honestly: we were talking about the sound the water made when we finally got it to surface."
> "Grower / beekeeper at the Cardinal Scale; saves her own seed."

**Short declaratives that carry a real invariant.**
> "The currency can raise a ceiling. It can never lower a floor."
> "We borrow steadiness from one another. We catch dysregulation from one another."
> "Every person had one. The plural is genii."

**Physical verbs and domestic images instead of noun stacks.**
> "It is not assembled and switched on; it is planted, watered, weeded, and given time"
> "No one strip-mines their own hand."
> "the difference between a prison wall and the wall of a home"

**Register drops and blunt candor at the right moment.**
> "Anyone who claims otherwise is selling something."
> "Donna is made."
> "We retire the headline."

Series-wide exemplars to calibrate against before each session: the Maren narrative (Vol III), the Donna frame (Vol V), the Cardinal Scale vignettes (Vol IV), the fishery and hollowing-institution passages (Vol II), the prologue's blue paint (Vol VIII), the Alu / Nilnisi / Ayan vignettes (Vols VIII and IX). These are the register the whole series is edited toward. Expand nothing; but when cutting slop, cut toward these.

## Protected inventory

### Canon terms, letter-perfect

Check every touched instance against the master ledger. Includes: Providence, PURPOSEFUL (and its ten-word acronym expansion), ICONS ("Individual Communities Observing Natural Sovereignty"), Scale, the Cardinal Scale, COHERENCE, the Currency of Presence, Bio-Consensus, the Four Streams, Proof of Council, the Membrane, the Coherence Engine, the Doors, the dragon named Earth, the Seven Initiates (with their Greek loves), the Twelve Tenets, the Master Human Arc, the pne(h)uma(n) Curriculum (exact typography), B.O.W. / Body of Worship, Coherent Resonance, the Living Constitution and the ten constitutional principles by name, presence vs. presencing (a technical pair, not a redundancy), the four evidence tiers (established, emerging, experimental, speculative), "right relationship", "the long now", "attention from within".

- "we may be wrong." (Vol VIII, bolded)

### Structural devices, per volume

- Separators differ by volume and must not be mixed or normalized: ".  :  ." (Vols I to III; never change internal spacing), "∗   ∗   ∗" (Vol V), "—  ·  —" (Vol VI; typography, exempt from the dash rule), "⁂" (Vol VII), "---" rules (Vol VIII). Vols IV and IX have none; do not introduce any.
- Recurring section apparatus: Vol IV's "The Deeper Inquiry", "What Remains Open", "Coda: In Plain Terms", and bolded "Toward the coherent substrate." paragraphs; Vol VII's italic "Within the Coherence Thesis." codas; Vol VI's four-line italic coda liturgy ("That is why."); Vol IX's five italic "We need the..." recruiting taglines; register notes ("A note on the register of this volume").
- The seed / sprout / stem / soil / flower developmental headings and named pauses (Vol I), the delayed-naming device (Vol V, announced in its preface), Part Two's retelling structure (Vol VI), the planet-per-volume arc.
- Epigraphs: self-quotes from other volumes stay verbatim here and get fixed at their source volume first, then reconciled. External-attribution epigraphs (Tainter, Ricoeur, Kimmerer, Rumi, Fuller, Krishnamurti) are exempt from all house rules; never fabricate exact wording or add citations.
- Tables (Vol VII's loves and touch tables, ledger tables, Appendix A in Vol VIII): edit prose cells lightly, never prosify the table.

### Always exempt

Poems and verse (Vol VII's dedication and closing poem, italic coda blocks functioning as verse), external quotations (including the Palantir, Thiel, and IARC quotes and all bolded statistics with their qualifiers), dictionary-style definitions, acronym blocks, numbers and doctrinal figures (spelled-out numbers are house style: "eighty-seven people", "a hundred and eight to a hundred and fifty"), character names (Maren, Donna, Mahlinah, Elan, Alu, Nilnisi, Ayan), and all heading text.

## Calibration rewrites

Study these before editing. Each preserves every claim and canon term while removing the machinery.

**Vol I, the volume's second sentence:**
> Before: "It argues that civilization's current crises are not primarily political, ideological, or technological failures, but biological oversights — and that any viable post-extractive future must be built on coherence as the substrate that sustains collective intelligence at planetary scale, where global interdependence, velocity, and total systemic entanglement exceed the capacity of traditional forms of coordination."
>
> After: "It argues that civilization's current crises are biological oversights before they are political or technological failures. Any viable post-extractive future must be built on coherence, the substrate that sustains collective intelligence at planetary scale. The world is now too interdependent, too fast, and too entangled for the old forms of coordination to hold it."

**Vol II, double denial-reveal:**
> Before: "A civilization is not an abstraction. It is the aggregate behavior of nervous systems coordinating with one another. When those nervous systems lose the capacity to regulate themselves, every system built on top of them inherits the dysregulation. This is not metaphor. It is mechanism."
>
> After: "A civilization is the aggregate behavior of nervous systems coordinating with one another. When those nervous systems lose the capacity to regulate themselves, every system built on top of them inherits the dysregulation. The claim is mechanical, not metaphorical."

**Vol III, throat-clearing before a claim:**
> Before: "We come now to the boldest claim of this chapter, and the one most exposed to misunderstanding. We make it deliberately, and we surround it with the safeguards that the governance chapters will detail, because without it the chain stops short of the ground and with it the chain reaches all the way down to the soil."
>
> After: "Here is the boldest claim in this chapter. Without it the chain stops short of the ground. With it, the chain reaches the soil. The governance chapters describe the safeguards around it."

**Vol IV, scaffolded seesaw:**
> Before: "The internal economy is not a secondary operational concern. It is the mechanism through which the constitutional principles either become real or remain aspirational. An institution that has articulated a constitutional commitment to regenerative participation but whose internal economy rewards extractive performance will, over time, be an extractive institution with regenerative rhetoric."
>
> After: "The internal economy is where the constitutional principles become real or stay words. An institution that professes regenerative participation while rewarding extractive performance becomes, over time, an extractive institution with regenerative rhetoric."

**Vol V, daisy-chain:**
> Before: "Once intentions become visible, relationships become possible. Once relationships become possible, collaboration becomes possible. Once collaboration becomes possible, contribution becomes possible at a scale and speed that isolation could never reach. Visibility is the hinge on which the whole sequence turns, and we have almost none of it for the things that matter most."
>
> After: "Visible intentions make relationships possible. Relationships make collaboration possible, and collaboration carries contribution to a scale isolation never reaches. Visibility is the hinge. We have almost none of it for the things that matter most."

**Vol VIII, muffled metaphor:**
> Before: "But fear is meant to be a *messenger,* not a *landlord.* It should knock, deliver its warning, be thanked, and dismissed — not move in and govern the house."
>
> After: "Fear is a messenger, not a landlord. It should knock, deliver its warning, and be thanked at the door. It should not move in and govern the house."

## Never do

- Never compress a narrative vignette into summary, or abstract a named person into a role.
- Never smooth a register drop or a blunt admission.
- Never add material to reach concreteness; concreteness comes from what the sentence already means.
- Never let a rewrite grow a new triad, a new dash, or a new intensifier. Re-lint your own output.
- Never edit quoted text from another volume at the quoting site; fix the source first.
- Never "fix" what volume-notes.md lists as flag-only: continuity bugs, citation-name errors, rename artifacts, attribution questions.
- Never introduce the series' separator or coda devices into a volume that does not use them.
