# Per-Volume Editorial Notes

Baseline numbers are from `npm run manuscripts:lint-prose` at audit time (July 2026). "Banned" counts prose instances only. Re-run the linter for current numbers before starting a pass.

## Global decisions needed before certain edits (author, not editor)

1. **Epigraph attribution dashes.** "— The Coherence Thesis, Vol. III" is conventional citation format used series-wide. Decide once whether the no-dash rule covers attribution lines; restyle all volumes in one decision, never ad hoc. The linter classifies these lines separately.
2. **Heading punctuation.** Vols VIII and IX use "§N — Title" headings; heading text anchors deep-link section IDs. Any change is a link-preservation event (aliases plus section ledger), done as its own PR, not inside a prose pass.
3. **Heading normalization series-wide.** Each volume invents its own heading grammar, and Vol VII has zero markdown headings (structure lives in bold paragraphs), so its sections cannot anchor deep links. Vols V and VI carry ALL-CAPS label paragraphs ("CHAPTER ONE") invisible to the toolchain. Normalizing is import-pipeline work with ledger entries, separate from editorial passes.
4. **Master ledger publication.** The ledger is a working canon register (pending decisions, verdict stamps, arrows) living in `sources/manuscripts/` and compiling with the reader volumes. Decide whether to exclude it from compilation or edit it to publishable form.
5. **Vol I draft blockquotes.** Production notes at lines 11 and 842 (Canon Sheet v1.0 / Canon v1.5 alignment). The closing note also records that the Master Human Arc is deliberately hidden; deleting these needs owner sign-off.
6. **Vol III [verify] markers.** Twelve unresolved citation placeholders, described in-text as deliberate honesty. Resolve, keep, or restyle: author's call before publication.

- A canon register, not reader prose. Most structure is load-bearing machinery: tables, decision letters, section numbers, verdict stamps. De-slop prose cells only; never prosify tables, renumber decisions, or retitle headings.

- Known internal inconsistency to reconcile: Decision G estimates "~20x" lowercase instances while §9 reports 37 applied.
- Its em dashes sit mostly in headers and tables (frozen pending global decisions 2 and 4).
