## Debt

The manuscripts make incompatible promises about material survival. One makes food, shelter, care, dignity, and belonging unconditional. Another ties material provision to self-regulation or a coherence-derived condition.

## Evidence

Volume I states that the currency may never lower the floor of survival. Revised Volume III now states that food, shelter, dignity, and belonging do not depend on physiology, presence, or usefulness, but that doctrinal correction still awaits author ratification and a real provisioning institution. Volume IX calls the floor unconditional, then says people earn it through self-coherence and that "we feed all who can hold themselves."

## Paydown criteria

Ratify or reject the unconditional rule. Define the material institution, funding, eligibility, jurisdiction, and remedies that can supply the floor. Separate universal provision from any earned additional stewardship, then revise every affected passage and protocol invariant consistently.
