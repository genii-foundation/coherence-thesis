## Debt

The corpus does not provide one dated, versioned inventory of what Providence and PURPOSEFUL presently are, what exists in research or pilot form, what remains proposed, and what has been retired. Detailed design language can make institutions, protocols, products, credentials, programs, governance bodies, and research relationships sound operational. The federation horizon also lacks an accountable dependency and milestone plan.

## Evidence

Volume II describes Providence's present state, future development, trust architecture, AI relationship, and builder invitation in several different tenses. Volume III gives detailed architecture at current lines 65, 343 to 385, 441 to 443, 591 to 633, 856 to 905, 1057 to 1157, and 1236 to 1350, while repeatedly acknowledging that much remains unbuilt. Its line 1075 calls a ten-to-fifteen-year federation horizon a scenario because dependencies and milestones are unspecified. Volumes IV, V, VII, and IX plus the master ledger and site make additional claims about construction, PURPOSEFUL programs, practices, the Cardinal Scale, and solved architecture. No shared record dates or versions those claims.

## Paydown criteria

Publish an inventory with one entry for every named entity, program, protocol, model, credential, governance body, product, research partnership, and service. Give each entry a canonical name, version, status such as conceptual, proposed, in research, piloted, active, paused, or retired, an accountable owner, supporting evidence, and a last-verified date. Link each manuscript claim and site surface to that record. For federation, define protocol versions, governance transfers, credential and privacy dependencies, adoption tests, milestones, stopping conditions, and accountable owners. Update the inventory whenever status changes and reconcile it with CTD-0030, CTD-0031, CTD-0047, CTD-0055, and CTD-0056.
