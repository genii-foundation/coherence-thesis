## Debt

Volume VII gives mentors substantial authority over readiness, depth, sequence, identity, and belonging without defining consent, complaint, safeguarding, qualification, independent oversight, or exit.

## Evidence

Current line 331 says a mentor cannot presence what they have not met in themselves. Lines 345 to 369 say the path creates a whole person, the order is not negotiable, mentors judge readiness, and teaching may be withheld until its hour. Lines 399 to 409 describe refuge, depth metering, councils, and restorative organs.

## Paydown criteria

Define informed consent, voluntary pacing, refusal without penalty, facilitator qualification, trauma boundaries, complaint and appeal, safeguarding, independent oversight, and exit. Remove claims of nonnegotiable sequence until those controls exist. Obtain qualified safeguarding review before any program or invitation is published as active.

## History

- 2026-07-09: Recorded as a critical rights and safeguarding obligation by the initial independent corpus audit.
- 2026-07-10: Added explicit consent, refusal, mentor change, complaint, appeal, exit, qualification, supervision, accessibility, and anti-isolation requirements; retained the critical gate for qualified safeguarding review.

## Partial paydown

The current source at `sources/manuscripts/coherence-thesis-vol7-presencing-genius.md` lines 323 to 329 and 357 to 393 now bounds mentor authority with demonstrated competence, scope, supervision, trauma-informed boundaries, accessibility, independent review, informed consent, refusal, mentor change, complaint, appeal, and exit without penalty. It prohibits compelled disclosure, isolation, spiritual authority claims, and shelter conditional on participation or mentor approval. `editorial/reviews/volume-7/production-pass/semantic-review.md` independently confirms the rights correction. The item remains critical and open because no qualified safeguarding specialist has reviewed the design, and no active program has demonstrated that it implements these controls.
