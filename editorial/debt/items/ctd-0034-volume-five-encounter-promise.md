## Debt

Volume IV promises that Volume V will be written from actual building experience and report what Providence produced in contact with reality. The published Volume V is instead a philosophical return to human purpose and potential, and it supplies no such implementation report.

## Evidence

Volume IV says Volume V cannot be written from the position of the first four books and must wait for experience from the building. Volume V opens by saying Volume IV showed the architecture can actually be built, then develops PURPOSEFUL and the builders without documenting a real build or identifying the promised empirical encounter.

## Paydown criteria

Choose whether Volume V should fulfill the promised encounter, whether that account belongs in a later work, or whether Volume IV made a promise the series should retract. Align both transitions without inventing implementation evidence and clearly label every design-fiction passage.
