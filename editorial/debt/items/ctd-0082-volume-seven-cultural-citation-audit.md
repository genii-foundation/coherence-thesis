## Debt

Volume VII claims authority from Latin, Greek, Taoist, cosmological, and other cultural traditions without a source register or enough distinction between historical account and the manuscript's own synthesis.

## Evidence

Current lines 135 to 153 associate Gaia with intelligence in the between, give a Latin account of genius and genii, and attribute the Four Windows of Perception to Taoist philosophy. Lines 441 to 459 closely echo language associated with Kahlil Gibran without attribution. The volume contains no complete source register.

## Paydown criteria

Verify primary texts, translations, etymology, and tradition-specific interpretations. Attribute adaptations clearly. Invite qualified review for Taoist and other living-tradition claims. Add a stable source apparatus that preserves the difference between inherited teaching and author synthesis.

## History

- 2026-07-09: Recorded by the initial independent corpus audit.
- 2026-07-10: Removed or narrowed unsupported lineage claims and unattributed adaptation during the production pass; qualified cultural and primary-source review remains outstanding.

## Partial paydown

The current Volume VII removes the unverified Taoist attribution for the Four Windows, presents the Seven Initiates and love pairings as a project synthesis, narrows the Roman genius account, and retires Gibran-adjacent closing language without adequate attribution. `editorial/reviews/volumes/volume-07/2026-07-09-production-pass/semantic-review.md` and `editorial/reviews/volumes/volume-07/2026-07-09-production-pass/literary-review.md` confirm that no lineage is being treated as proof. The full primary-text, translation, etymology, attribution, and living-tradition review required by this item has not occurred, so it remains open.
