## Debt

Volume III offers a concrete constitutional sketch for Providence, but its entity structure, revenue transfers, chambers, representation rules, authority migration, audits, sunset clauses, and dissolution mechanisms remain placeholders. Existing debt covers biometric standing and technical safeguards, but no item owns review of the constitution as a complete legal and democratic system.

## Evidence

Current lines 856 to 901 propose a Foundation and public-benefit enterprise, five and ten percent revenue transfers, seven Stewardship Council seats, a forty percent investor cap, participant equality, authority migration, complaint and appeal systems, independent audits, and conditions for pause or dissolution. The source labels several figures provisional. The semantic review requires author ratification of equal political voice, the literary review defers cadence work until governance positions are approved, and the slop review requires jurisdiction-specific legal and democratic review.

## Paydown criteria

Complete jurisdiction-specific legal review, power mapping, minority safeguards, conflict and appeal design, fiduciary analysis, data-governance analysis, and a review of capture and sabotage risks. State which bodies exist, which remain proposals, and what authority each may exercise. Source, model, or remove unsupported numeric structures. Obtain author approval and reconcile the result with CTD-0005, CTD-0006, CTD-0040, and CTD-0055.
