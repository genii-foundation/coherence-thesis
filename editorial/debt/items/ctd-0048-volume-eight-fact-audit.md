## Debt

Volume VIII relies on a dense, time-sensitive factual accounting of ecology, public health, war, economics, artificial intelligence, law, climate, genetics, surveillance, fertility, loneliness, and sexual behavior. Its roots section is a reading list rather than a complete claim-to-source apparatus, and the manuscript itself says the mid-2026 figures must be refreshed before publication.

## Evidence

The volume makes precise claims about bee losses, agricultural value, insect biomass, nutrition and cancer, glyphosate, microplastics, active conflicts, deaths and displacement, inflation, AI labor effects, climate, overdose, suicide, 23andMe, social credit, fertility, sperm counts, government transparency, and intimacy. It also presents claims about seven planetary lights, weekday naming, and direct transformations among solstice, Christmas, Samhain, Halloween, Imbolc, and Groundhog Day without adequate historical boundaries. Several citations are secondary organizations or news summaries, and many entries lack authors, exact titles, dates, links, methods, or direct attachment to the sentence they support.

## Paydown criteria

Create a claim-level source map, verify every current fact against primary or authoritative sources, date-stamp volatile claims, attach citations to exact wording, distinguish contested findings, and obtain subject review for medical and legal claims. Use historical scholarship to distinguish symbolic analogy from cultural descent and narrow claims by language, region, and period. Remove any figure or genealogy that cannot survive the check, then refresh the audit immediately before publication.
