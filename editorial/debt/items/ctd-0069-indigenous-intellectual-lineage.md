## Debt

Volume III draws an institutional lesson from diverse Indigenous nations and communities without naming the traditions that actually informed Providence or documenting review, consent, attribution, or reciprocity. Factual accuracy alone cannot establish an accountable intellectual relationship.

## Evidence

Current lines 301 to 305 acknowledge diversity and the danger of extraction, then describe a common challenge to modern institutions and say Providence draws from that lesson. The semantic review requires informed review for Indigenous-governance claims. The slop review separately finds that the passage names no nation or intellectual lineage and records no consent or material reciprocity.

## Paydown criteria

Identify the people, nations, texts, and traditions that actually informed the proposal. Obtain appropriate review or consent. Provide precise attribution and material reciprocity where applicable. Preserve differences among traditions and remove any pan-Indigenous synthesis the evidence cannot support. If no defensible lineage can be established, remove the inheritance claim rather than using a generalized tradition as moral authority.
