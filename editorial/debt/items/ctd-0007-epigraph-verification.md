## Debt

Named epigraphs and quotations in Volumes II, III, IV, VII, and IX have not been checked against primary editions, and the manuscripts do not consistently distinguish exact quotation from adaptation. Volume II also attributes nine self-epigraphs to Volume I even though several do not appear to match its current wording exactly.

## Evidence

The Volume II lead review found nine self-epigraphs whose status as exact quotation or adaptation remains unclear. Volume III now labels thirteen false exact self-attributions as adaptations, but its independent literary review found a nearly uniform apparatus of twenty-one `After` or `Adapted from` chapter openings. Volumes IV and VII add unchecked epigraphs and cultural attributions. Volume IX adds self-epigraphs and a Buckminster Fuller quotation that is often circulated without a reliable primary source. Editorial punctuation rules cannot justify altering a quotation while presenting it as exact, and verified attribution does not by itself justify repetitive borrowed authority.

## Paydown criteria

Check every epigraph against a primary or authoritative edition, preserve exact wording where quoted, mark adaptations explicitly, and record complete source details. Then review each volume's epigraph sequence as a literary apparatus, retaining attribution only where it earns the opening rather than manufacturing uniform gravity.
