## Debt

Volume III uses the Douro and several institutional lineages as historical support without a completed source audit.

## Evidence

The Douro frame makes claims about local knowledge, dams, migration, river management, and community loss. The chapter on lineage makes compressed claims about churches, nation-states, corporations, universities, platforms, monasteries, guilds, Indigenous governance, mutual aid, scientific communities, Quaker process, stewardship trusts, and the commons. The epilogue and appendix also retain an unchecked account of Aldo Leopold's composition and publication history.

## Paydown criteria

Verify each historical example with regional primary or scholarly sources, distinguish analogy from evidence, correct overgeneralization, and name traditions precisely enough to avoid appropriation or false continuity. Verify the Leopold edition and biographical facts. Handle consent, reciprocity, and accountable Indigenous lineage under CTD-0069.
