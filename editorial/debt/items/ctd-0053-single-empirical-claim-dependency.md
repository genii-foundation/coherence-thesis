## Debt

Volume I says the entire architecture rests on a single empirical claim about detecting genuine presence. Detectability is load-bearing, but the design also depends on anti-counterfeiting, privacy, security, governance, incentives, voluntariness, cultural and disability fairness, institutional uptake, material provision, and ecological viability.

## Evidence

The phrase appears in both the falsifiability section and final burden of proof. Volume I also asserts measurement before admitting that the relevant detection remains unproven. Later volumes explicitly enumerate many independent failure conditions, so the singular dependency compresses a large system into one convenient hinge.

## Paydown criteria

Build an explicit dependency map separating the first empirical test from every empirical, technical, constitutional, social, and normative assumption. Retain `single` only if the manuscript can defend the exact scope it names.
