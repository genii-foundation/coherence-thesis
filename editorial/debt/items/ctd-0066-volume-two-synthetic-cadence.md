## Debt

False antithesis, synthetic symmetry, repeated long-paragraph and short-verdict movement, abstract noun clusters, and a narrow set of prestige terms still give parts of Volume II a generated cadence. Many contrasts and domain terms are necessary. Their cumulative distribution makes distinct thoughts sound as though they came from one rhetorical mold.

## Evidence

The final slop audit reports 326 triad or longer-list candidates and 80 abstract noun clusters. Dense false contrast and symmetry remain at current lines 59, 111 to 147, 175 to 193, 229 to 235, 321 to 341, 389 to 415, 577, 601 to 659, 679, 745 to 747, 801 to 817, 853 to 857, 1019 to 1041, 1057 to 1063, and 1187. Abstract or lexically narrow passages remain at lines 77 to 99, 127 to 149, 369 to 439, 553 to 659, 839 to 947, 1003 to 1063, 1089 to 1101, and 1149 to 1187. The recurring long argument, short verdict, and enlarged conclusion pattern appears throughout the same sections.

## Paydown criteria

Read every retained contrast and parallel sequence aloud. Keep a contrast only when both alternatives are real and the distinction advances the argument. For each abstract passage, identify the actor, action, object, scale, and consequence. Preserve `coherence`, `substrate`, `infrastructure`, `field`, `threshold`, `civilization`, and `builders` only where each names a defined distinction. Vary paragraph movement according to thought, not for decorative variety. Rerun literary and complete slop review after the pass.
