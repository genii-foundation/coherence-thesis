## Debt

The corpus can be read as making two different ontological claims. One says a unit of COHERENCE is literally the relation made between people. The other treats the unit as a portable attestation or memory of that relation.

## Evidence

Volume I says COHERENCE is not a token for coherence, while the same chapter describes a self-sovereign record that carries a thin attestation. Volume II calls COHERENCE a unit and record of demonstrated capacity, then says coherence is not a score, does not accumulate, and is better understood as circulation. It also uses `physiological-wallet layer` without deciding whether wallet is a canonical container metaphor. Volume III distinguishes the Currency, unit, record, presence, and presencing, but still leaves what can travel unclear. Volume IV describes export and portability. Volume IX describes COHERENCE as accumulating in a wallet called Coherent Resonance while also saying the long-horizon portable store of value remains unsolved. The distinctions affect minting, ownership, transfer, privacy, revocation, and the truth of the currency metaphor.

## Paydown criteria

Choose and define the relation among lived presencing, measured signal, attestation, unit, record, wallet, ownership, circulation, portability, and transfer. Decide whether wallet is the intended container metaphor. Apply the definitions consistently wherever COHERENCE is introduced or implemented, and test them against the record lifecycle in CTD-0054.
