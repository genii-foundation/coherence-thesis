## Debt

Volume II contains load-bearing scientific, historical, quantitative, technical, institutional, and current-event claims without a reproducible source apparatus. Its no-footnotes argument treats verification as secondary for builders even though the argument depends on checkable evidence.

## Evidence

The final reviews identify claims about physiology, polyvagal theory, HRV, cortisol, co-regulation, trauma, empathy, Pol.is, assemblies, ecological monitoring, Indigenous stewardship, RLHF, Constitutional AI, agricultural surveys, Sardex, Mondragon, vTaiwan, deliberative outcomes, funding comparisons, organizations, and four proposed physiological channels. Several named organizations or deployments may drift. Several universal claims compress contested research. Naming a project does not make the supporting claim reproducible.

## Paydown criteria

Inventory every material claim and verify it against primary or authoritative sources. Record dates, populations, comparison classes, uncertainty, methods, and counterevidence. Obtain qualified cultural, Indigenous-governance, disability, neurodiversity, trauma, and biometric-ethics review where relevant. Add an unobtrusive source apparatus, such as chapter endnotes, a maintained source guide, or a linked bibliography. Revise the no-footnotes explanation so accessibility does not diminish verification, and qualify or remove claims whose force exceeds the evidence.
