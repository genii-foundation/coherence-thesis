## Debt

The revised Reader's Part gives readers full interpretive autonomy, while the Final Note still asserts that some part of the reader already recognizes the work being asked of them. The two closings now use incompatible theories of reader consent.

## Evidence

The Reader's Part allows agreement, resistance, uncertainty, or partial reception. Other passages still predict feelings, assign grief, declare perceptions accurate, tell readers which work is theirs, and imply moral obligation. The baseline was consistently more conscripting, so the revised posture is a substantive philosophical change rather than neutral copyediting.

## Paydown criteria

Choose an autonomous, conscripting, or deliberately intermediate reader relationship. Separate witness from diagnosis, invite action without claiming access to the reader's inner state, align the full direct-address sequence, and record explicit author approval.
