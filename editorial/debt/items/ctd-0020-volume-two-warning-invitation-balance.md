## Debt

The revised volume retains strong warnings about civilizational danger while ending in a more open invitation. Only the author can decide whether the published voice should carry this degree of certainty and urgency.

## Evidence

The tonal choice is concentrated in Chapters One, Ten, Twelve, Thirteen, and the Final Note. Residual passages declare that a window is closing, the work is not optional, or a reader's perception is accurate. Several chapter endings also rise toward builders, fragments, and irreversible thresholds. The prose is internally coherent, but editorial judgment cannot determine the desired emotional pressure of the final work.

## Paydown criteria

Review the named chapters and closing crescendos as one tonal sequence. Choose the intended warning-to-invitation ratio, distinguish warning from moral conscription, apply any resulting changes consistently, and record author approval in the Volume II review.
