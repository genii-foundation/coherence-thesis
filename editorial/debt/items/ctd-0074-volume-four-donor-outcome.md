## Debt

The coda to Volume IV's donor chapter states the opposite of the chapter's narrated outcome. The chapter says the Cardinal Scale loses the offered money. The coda says the community accepted it.

## Evidence

Current lines 1427 to 1435 say the Cardinal Scale declines a five-million-dollar offer, proposes compatible terms, and loses the gift when the donor declines the counteroffer. Line 1454 says the community accepted the gift gladly and entirely on its own terms.

## Paydown criteria

Decide whether the gift was declined, accepted after renegotiation, or replaced by a different gift. Revise the narrative and coda to one outcome, then check every later reference to the event.

## History

- 2026-07-09: Recorded by the initial independent corpus audit.
- 2026-07-10: Resolved after the Volume IV production pass and fresh independent semantic review confirmed one consistent donor outcome throughout the revised source.

## Resolution

The revised donor sequence at `sources/manuscripts/coherence-thesis-vol4-architecting-providence.md` lines 1125 to 1145 now has one outcome. The community offers a counterproposal, the donor declines it, and the Scale loses the five-million-dollar gift. No later passage says the gift was accepted. `editorial/reviews/volume-4/production-pass/semantic-review.md` records the baseline contradiction, the corrected sequence, and the independent comparison. `editorial/reviews/volume-4/production-pass/overcompression-audit.md` confirms that the donor chapter retained the conditions, process, refusal, and material cost while the contradictory coda was retired.
