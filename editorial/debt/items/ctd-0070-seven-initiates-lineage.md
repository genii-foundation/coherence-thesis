## Debt

The Seven Initiates are presented as an ordered, cumulative developmental map drawn from several traditions, but the corpus does not show their derivation, justify the sequence, or explain their relationship to the seven named forms of love. Readers cannot tell which elements are inherited, synthesized, renamed, or original to the project.

## Evidence

Volume III lines 255 to 275 define Listening, Intimacy, Balance, Art & Alchemy, Embodiment, Worship, and Providence as cumulative thresholds that are entered in order. The passage calls the categories partial and says they draw from several traditions without naming those sources. Volume VII supplies canonical sequence claims. The semantic review corrected the sequence to match Volume VII, the literary review restored the canonical `Art & Alchemy` label, and the slop review found the lineage and scope still unproved.

## Paydown criteria

Produce a cross-volume lineage note that identifies the source, adaptation, or original rationale for every term and for the sequence itself. Verify any claim of relationship to forms of love or other traditions. Preserve `Art & Alchemy` unless an approved canon decision changes it. Distinguish a working developmental map from a validated taxonomy, obtain author approval of its scope, and align Volume III, Volume VII, and the master ledger.
