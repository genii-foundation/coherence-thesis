## Debt

The baseline conclusion treats coherence as the governing constraint on civilization's technological power. The revision treats it as one of two factors and says it may govern the other.

## Evidence

The weaker conclusion can conflict with the Seed's claim that coherence is a constraint the design must satisfy. Volume II repeatedly says technology does not choose and conditions choose, while its own architecture chapters argue that technical affordances constrain behavior. The handoff risks treating technology as a neutral amplifier and denying the design power the manuscript later relies on.

## Paydown criteria

Choose the governing-constraint thesis or the two-factor thesis. State the narrower interaction among technical affordances, ownership, incentives, institutions, and human choices. Align the Volume I Orientation, Seed, Stem, conclusion, and the complete Volume II argument with that decision.
