## Debt

Volume III leaves unclear whether a COHERENCE record derived partly from physiological signals can affect governance standing, stewardship authority, chamber eligibility, or decision weight.

## Evidence

The ambiguity sits beside strong promises that Providence is not social credit, does not rank people, and preserves an unconditional floor. Revised Volume III now begins political voice from equal standing, while still allowing participant-controlled evidence to inform bounded stewardship. Volume IV says no sensor decides who may speak, vote, or lead. Volume IX says presencing earns "the standing to lead and to steward." Governance consequences could violate equal standing and the stated invariants even if participation is voluntary.

## Paydown criteria

Ratify equal political voice. State which bounded stewardship decisions may consider attestations, which may never do so, and what membership, evidence control, non-biometric routes, due process, appeal, anti-discrimination, and measurement-error protections apply.
