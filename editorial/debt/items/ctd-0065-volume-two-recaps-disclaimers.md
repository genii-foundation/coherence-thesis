## Debt

Volume II repeatedly explains the fishery, money's blindness, Providence's limitations, and the current minimum viable proposal. Some recurrence protects readers from mistaking design for implementation. The accumulated recaps and nearly identical disclaimers nevertheless make the volume feel defensive and templated.

## Evidence

The literary review identifies repeated Providence openings at current lines 571, 709 to 713, 779 to 783, 845, and 933, including repeated use of `This is a modest and more defensible claim.` The slop review traces the larger recap pattern through current lines 305 to 327, 387 to 473, 571 to 573, 593 to 599, 639 to 647, 655 to 713, 779 to 785, 845 to 849, 933 to 937, and 1107 to 1117. It also records exact duplicates at lines 401 and 461, 597 and 675, 573 and 849, and 339 and 867.

## Paydown criteria

Build a developmental repetition map for the complete volume. Mark each recurrence as cumulative, independently necessary, or redundant. Keep the strongest first statement, preserve one concise implementation disclaimer wherever the risk genuinely returns, and remove or recast every recap that does not change scale, meaning, evidence, or consequence. Confirm through semantic review that consolidation has not made a proposal sound operational.
