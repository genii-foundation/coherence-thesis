## Debt

The corpus alternates among 30 to 150, approximately 150, approximately 500, and nested subgroups as the population scale at which direct relational coherence can hold.

## Evidence

Volume IV says communities significantly above 150 cannot sustain direct relational coherence, then says a Cardinal Scale near 500 remains small enough that no one becomes a stranger. Volume V calls approximately 150 the natural size of a Scale. Volume VI repeats roughly 150 as the relational size. Volume IX distinguishes a human scale near 150 from a bioregional size near 500 without consistently explaining whether the larger community is one relational unit or a federation of nested units.

## Paydown criteria

Define the direct-trust threshold, the governance unit, and the full Scale population as separate concepts if that distinction is intended. Establish the evidence and uncertainty behind each range, explain the nesting mechanism, and use the same terms and numbers across the corpus.
