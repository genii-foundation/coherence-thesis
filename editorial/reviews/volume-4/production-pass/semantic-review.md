## Claim invariants

The revision must preserve these propositions:

1. Providence is a proposed coordination architecture, not a verified operating institution.
2. Construction must begin with sequence. Culture, trust, and constitutional limits should precede platform, scale, and concentrated power.
3. A first community must test the architecture under real stakes, with informed consent, visible subsidy, dissent, appeal, succession, amendment, and exit.
4. Growth changes the form of coordination. Smaller direct-trust groups and larger nested communities are distinct design levels whose population limits remain provisional.
5. Economics is constitutional. Legal form, ownership, revenue, capital terms, measurement, compensation, and concentration all distribute power.
6. Shared infrastructure requires ownership, governance, maintenance, fork, privacy, and remedy. Calling something common does not settle those questions.
7. Interpretation, amendment, stewardship, anti-capture, divergence, and exit require procedures that do not depend on founder restraint.
8. Identity and trust infrastructure must remain portable, consent based, appealable, and resistant to social rank. Physiology cannot establish civic standing or character.
9. AI may support sensemaking, translation, matching, and procedure. It cannot determine trustworthiness or make binding governance decisions.
10. The first application is a constitutional specification. Its controls are requirements, not implemented facts.
11. The institution owes workers and participants material support, safe conditions, anti-retaliation, practical exit, and repair.
12. The developmental arc is a heuristic for support, not a universal rank or eligibility system.
13. Part VII is design fiction. Its watershed, people, donor, chronology, and outcomes are imagined tests of mechanisms.
14. In the donor scenario, the community counters, the donor declines, and the community loses the gift.
15. Adaptive threat modeling must not turn critics, dissenters, or newcomers into presumed enemies.
16. The dragon is a bounded image for distributed relation, not biological proof, a universal cultural doctrine, or evidence of a planetary mind.
17. The invitation is reciprocal and freely declined. Reading does not enroll a person in a network.
