## Authorial profile

- Central claims: Coherence is a biological condition of durable coordination. A post-extractive civilization must preserve that condition at scale. Providence, PURPOSEFUL, the Currency of Presence, and sovereign Scales form one falsifiable proposal for doing so.
- Register and point of view: Plain philosophical argument with shifts into biological explanation, organic metaphor, narrative example, institutional design, and direct invitation.
- Characteristic cadence: Compact propositions alternate with longer cumulative paragraphs. The argument descends from Seed to Soil, then rises into the Flower.
- Recurring images and motifs: Seed, sprout, stem, soil, flower, ground, roots, flame, connective tissue, cells becoming a body, rooms returning to themselves, trust traveling, and tending.
- Protected terms: coherence; Providence; PURPOSEFUL; Currency of Presence; COHERENCE; Bio-Consensus; the Doors; ICONS; Scale; Cardinal Scale.
- Protected lines or passages: The core Seed claim; the bridge-load analogy; "The technology does not choose. The conditions surrounding it choose."; "It is wisdom exchanged for future."; the surveillance invariants; Maren's frightened-room vignette; "Before a thousand, one."; the final seed line.
- Known uncertainties: Empirical detectability, group-performance research, several historical priority claims, the uniqueness of the proposed network, and the precise ontological relation between COHERENCE and the relation it records.

## Representative comparisons

### Opening claim

Before:

> The Coherence Thesis is an invitation to recognize that the future of civilization will not be decided by technology or ideology, but by whether we learn to organize society around the biological conditions that make intelligence, trust, and coordination possible at planetary scale.

After:

> The Coherence Thesis begins with a constraint: civilization cannot coordinate at planetary scale unless its people can sustain intelligence and trust under pressure. Technology and ideology matter, but neither can override the biological conditions that make coordination possible.

Why: The revision begins with the constraint, preserves technology and ideology as real factors, and removes the inflated prediction about what will decide the future.

### Providence

Before:

> So Providence is best understood not as a government, a company, a social network, or a technology platform. Those are mechanisms it might use. It is closer to this: the forward-facing architecture of a larger effort, the application through which people find one another and the trust between them can actually move.

After:

> Providence is not itself a government, company, social network, or technology platform, though it may use some of their mechanisms. It is the forward-facing architecture of a larger effort, an application through which people find one another and trust can move among them.

Why: The revision keeps the boundary and function while removing throat clearing and the vague intensifier.

### Maren

Before:

> And then the network does the one thing she could never do for herself. It lets that earned trust travel. A community three valleys over is coming apart over a hard decision, and it does not need money or a consultant. It needs exactly what Maren is.

After:

> The network then does what Maren could not do alone. It lets her earned trust travel. A community three valleys away is coming apart over a hard decision. It needs neither money nor a consultant. It needs what Maren can do.

Why: The revision protects the scene and its cadence while replacing a claim about what Maren is with the capacity she can bring.
