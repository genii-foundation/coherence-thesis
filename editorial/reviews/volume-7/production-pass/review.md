## Developmental map

1. Parts I and II orient the reader to the preceding volumes and identify the planetary scheme as literary symbolism rather than evidence.
2. Part III defines the internal technologies while separating lived relation from a technical record.
3. Part IV treats presence as a possible support for coordination and explicitly rejects biometric or charismatic proof of wisdom.
4. Part V preserves the Seven Initiates while presenting them as a working map rather than a universal sequence.
5. Part VI retains all Twelve Tenets and distinguishes spiritual posture, metaphor, observable behavior, and unsupported metaphysical claim.
6. Part VII establishes the mentor safeguards missing from the baseline.
7. Part VIII reframes the year as a curriculum sketch, keeps the touch example, and makes sequence adaptable rather than compulsory.
8. Part IX keeps embodied retreat practice while requiring accessibility, refusal, privacy, and trauma-aware design.
9. Part X preserves the community bodies as proposals and adds governance, due-process, and survivor protections.
10. The Coda makes freedom, consent, and correction part of the praxis rather than administrative footnotes.
