## Authorial profile

- Central claims: civilization needs trustworthy ways to coordinate wisdom as technical capability grows; Providence is one provisional architecture for doing so; human matching, practice, participant sovereignty, and a speculative Currency of Presence form its center; physiological signals may support reflection but cannot establish wisdom, worth, trust, or truth.
- Register and point of view: humane technical prose with philosophical gravity. First person plural names the builders' commitments. Third person analysis describes mechanisms and risks. Maren's design fiction receives close narrative attention without being presented as evidence.
- Characteristic cadence: compact propositions followed by longer causal or qualifying movement. Paragraphs tend to move through claim, limit, mechanism, and consequence.
- Recurring images and motifs: the nest, the Douro, water returning to daylight, membrane, bread, witness, threshold, participant held records, descendants, and a system refusing the throne.
- Protected terms: coherence; Providence; PURPOSEFUL; the Currency of Presence; COHERENCE; Bio-Consensus; Proof of Council; the Four Streams; the Coherence Engine; the Reflective Mirror; the Membrane; the three Doors; ICONS; Scale; the Cardinal Scale.

## Developmental map

1. Continuity carries the nest forward and defines providence and imperative without conscripting the reader.
2. Part One names coordination failure, defines coherence under pressure, and examines what inherited institutions held and cost.
3. Part Two identifies the conditions of wisdom, traces the proposed lineage, and introduces the three Doors, the Membrane, and the Currency of Presence.
4. Part Three separates four tiers of evidence, bounds the Four Streams, and makes the Coherence Engine answerable to participant interpretation.
5. Part Four tests the design through Maren, then asks how relational value could touch material life without making subsistence conditional on biometric approval.
6. Part Five treats governance as a constitutional problem, catalogs failure modes, and draws bright lines against social credit.
7. Part Six argues for protocol, openness, local compute, and federation while naming the threats those choices cannot defeat.
8. Part Seven makes the institution accountable for energy, materials, land, and actual ecological effects.
9. Part Eight proposes a versioned beginning, bounded research, honest financing, and an economic hypothesis instead of an inevitable market.
10. The epilogue returns to Leopold, the Douro, the people not yet born, and the difference between a map and evidence.
11. The appendix becomes an evidence map and publication blocking verification queue.

## Author queries

1. Question: Is the material floor unconditionally guaranteed, regardless of regulation, participation, usefulness, or any coherence record?
   - Location: "The Floor and the Height," "Six Bright Lines," and governance safeguards.
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: the baseline tied some material provision to self regulation while Volume I says survival cannot be lowered by the currency. The revision resolves the conflict toward unconditional dignity, which changes institutional doctrine.
   - Available options: approve the unconditional floor; define a narrower set of unconditional goods and revise both volumes; or explicitly reject biometric conditionality while distinguishing voluntary earned roles and additional resources.

2. Question: May any coherence record affect political standing, decision weight, chamber eligibility, or stewardship authority?
   - Location: "The Person Remains Sovereign," "Three Chambers," "Fourth Link," and "The Floor and the Height."
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: biometric influence over political voice conflicts with equal standing and the bright lines against social credit. The revision forbids weighting basic political voice and allows relational evidence to inform only bounded stewardship decisions with due process and non-biometric paths.
   - Available options: approve the current boundary; prohibit coherence evidence from every governance role; or define a narrow attestation use with appeal, expiry, disability protection, and independent review.

3. Question: What exactly does COHERENCE record, and what evidence creates a unit in Maren's vignette?
   - Location: "The Currency of Presence," "Presence and Presencing," "The Record," and Maren's year.
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: the canon names Bio-Consensus as the primary biometric minting path and Proof of Council as ancillary, while some passages describe testimony, commitments, or relational standing without identifying the mechanism.
   - Available options: define COHERENCE as a participant controlled attestation of a relational event; define it as the event itself; or specify a reviewed combination of Bio-Consensus, participant account, and Proof of Council.

4. Question: Which named epigraphs are exact quotations, which are adaptations, and which should become original series epigraphs?
   - Location: chapter and part display matter across the volume.
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: the wording has not been checked against primary editions. Presenting an adaptation as a quotation would be false attribution.
   - Available options: verify and quote exactly; retain the current "After" or "Adapted from" labels; or remove the external attribution.

5. Question: Should the scientific mechanism be published as a research proposal with the current evidence tiers, or should publication wait for a sourced technical review?
   - Location: Parts Three and Six, plus the appendix.
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: eighteen verification markers remain. The integrated Four Streams have not been validated, and several original claims about oxytocin, cortisol, pulse morphology, vocal state, HRV, synchrony, and neurodivergence exceeded the evidence shown.
   - Available options: complete a primary source review before publication; publish the mechanism explicitly as a research agenda; or remove the least supported pulse and endocrine material from the main argument.

6. Question: How much historical specificity should the Douro and institutional lineage retain before the source audit is complete?
   - Location: "The Central Wound," "Inheritance," the Earth Compact, and the epilogue.
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: the Douro, monasteries, guilds, Indigenous governance, mutual aid, Quaker process, and commons are used as lineage. Compressed lineage can become appropriation or false continuity.
   - Available options: commission a historical source pass; retain the current bounded analogies; or reduce the chapter to explicit design lessons without descent language.

7. Question: Which quantitative planning figures should remain visible before a model exists?
   - Location: endocrine roadmap, protocol horizon, ecological accounting, founding budget, five year financing, revenue contribution, participant stages, and Maren's imagined outcomes.
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: the figures may guide real decisions but currently lack methods, assumptions, and primary sources. Fluency is not a spreadsheet, however much a paragraph may yearn for venture capital.
   - Available options: build and cite the models; retain the numbers as labeled hypotheses; or remove them until substantiated.

8. Question: Is the personal companion a core strategic direction or one optional experiment?
   - Location: "Second Niche," "Why It Remains Unbuilt," and "An Economic Hypothesis."
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: the baseline treated it as an inevitable vast market. The revision treats it as one bounded experiment whose value must exceed privacy, dependency, bystander, and coercion risks.
   - Available options: approve the optional framing; restore it as a central strategy with evidence; or remove the companion and begin only with facilitated gatherings.

9. Question: Does the phrase "the ancestors are watching" carry the intended theology and temporal direction?
   - Location: continuity material and the epilogue.
   - Why intent cannot be inferred safely: the series uses it as a refrain, but the baseline shifted between literal, metaphorical, and future generation meanings.
