## Complete comparison result

### Meaning and modality

The revision preserves the volume's main thesis while narrowing its claim force. The baseline often said Providence detects genuine presence, makes trust immediate, resists gaming, solves coordination, and can finance itself. The revision now says the instruments may contribute evidence, trust remains provisional, gaming is unsolved, the currency is speculative, and the economic engine is a hypothesis. Those are necessary corrections, not losses of meaning.

No remaining line was found to convert a baseline possibility into an unsupported certainty. Several current claims still require sources or author approval, but the prose generally marks them as proposals, scenarios, placeholders, or tests.

### Causality

The baseline presented a direct causal chain from nervous-system regulation to food. The revision correctly separates the links. Regulation can widen attention, perception can support coordination, a network can improve discovery, and relational evidence can inform stewardship. Material provision still depends on law, land, labor, capital, infrastructure, and political power. The revised chain is defensible as a hypothesis. It is no longer described as proof.

### Scientific posture

The revision repairs the largest semantic failure in the baseline. It separates established, emerging, experimental, and speculative claims. It rejects HRV as a moral score, refuses to translate synchrony automatically into presencing, treats pulse and endocrine inference as experimental, and places participant interpretation inside the evidence rather than beneath it.

Publication still requires primary-source verification and expert review. The evidence appendix is a queue, not a finished scholarly apparatus.

### Governance and material rights

The baseline contradicted itself. It called the floor unconditional in its social credit safeguards while also making self-regulation the condition for food and shelter. It rejected ranking while weighting political voice through the coherence record. The revision resolves both contradictions in the safer direction. Survival is not earned through Bio-Consensus, and political voice begins from equal standing.

These are substantive changes to doctrine and governance. They are semantically coherent and align with the bright lines in Volumes I and IV, but they still need explicit author approval before merge.

### Narrative and chronology

Maren is now identified as a composite design-fiction character, and her outcomes are labeled hypotheses rather than results. One impossible chronology remained: a daughter who was nine when the collaborative formed could not, at forty, have never known the watershed as broken. That sentence is corrected below.

The vignette still needs a clear place in the versioned roadmap. It depicts all four streams, experimental pulse morphology, and the beginning of a coherence record, while the roadmap postpones accumulated standing until Providence 4.0.

### Cross-volume canon

The revision correctly preserves these relationships:

- COHERENCE is the proposed unit of the Currency of Presence.
- Bio-Consensus and the Four Streams name the primary proposed biometric path.
- Proof of Council is ancillary.
- Providence is connective tissue between Scales, not an authority over a Scale.
- Maren's watershed is distinct from the Cardinal Scale.
- ICONS is plural, and one community is a Scale.

I corrected the Seven Initiates to match Volume VII. They are cumulative thresholds entered in order, not independent domains.

The wider corpus still conflicts over the ontology of COHERENCE, the meaning of Coherent Resonance, the status of Proof of Council, the material floor, and whether the Cardinal Scale is composite, current, or already proved.

## Residual semantic risk

The manuscript's strongest remaining risk is category drift. A useful reflection can become standing. Standing can become currency. Currency can become resource access. Resource access can become governance power. The revised text names these transitions more honestly, but it does not yet define all the boundaries that keep one category from silently becoming the next.

The second risk is cross-volume certainty. Volume III now calls Bio-Consensus and COHERENCE proposals. Volumes IV and IX sometimes speak as though the measurement, wallet, community, and mint already work. A corpus harmonization pass must move later volumes toward the same evidentiary posture unless the missing evidence is supplied.
