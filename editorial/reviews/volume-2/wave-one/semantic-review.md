## Semantic integrity results

### Scope and modality

Present fact, design intent, possibility, and open research are generally kept distinct. The main drift occurred in the early Providence description and the lunar-cadence benefit. Both are corrected. The five-layer Reference Model now agrees with the five layers actually enumerated.

### Causality and logic

Two causal links were weakened or strengthened during compression. Both are corrected at lines 269 and 281. I found no other removed premise that breaks the local argument. Many causal claims remain in need of evidence, but they were inherited rather than created by this edit.

### Chronology and referents

No changed date or sequence was silently altered. The Ireland referent at line 755 is corrected. Historical dates, project counts, and current institutional claims still require external verification.

### Factual posture

The revision generally preserves whether a claim is report, inference, proposal, or aspiration. The strongest empirical and civilizational claims remain unsourced by deliberate design. That choice does not make their wording self-verifying. CTD-0019 must remain open until the factual pass tests each claim against authoritative sources.

### Terminology and cross-volume canon

Bio-Consensus, the Currency of Presence, and COHERENCE are now distinct in the Volume II source. Providence remains coordination infrastructure rather than a community or governing authority. ICONS remains plural. The unresolved question is whether COHERENCE names the relation itself or a portable record of that relation. That decision is already carried by CTD-0003.

### Compression and omitted meaning

The substantial compression usually removes scaffolding rather than propositions. I found no lost counterargument in Chapters One through Ten or Chapter Thirteen. The exception is the rewritten Reader's Part, where the change is philosophically and rhetorically substantive enough to require author approval.

## Blocking author decision

### Reader autonomy versus conscripted recognition

The baseline at the opening of the Reader's Part claimed that a reader had either been changed by the book or had refused to be changed. It also claimed that readers already perceived the conditions and could feel the work being asked of them. Current lines 1045 to 1049 replace that posture with reader autonomy: readers may agree, resist, remain uncertain, or carry only parts of the argument, and they decide what the account clarifies.

That is a meaningful philosophical improvement in one reading, but it is not a neutral recast. It changes the book's asserted relationship to its reader. Current line 1143 then returns to the stronger posture by telling the reader that some part of them recognizes the work being asked. The two closings now hold different theories of reader consent.

Paydown requires an author choice:

1. Keep the Reader's Part as revised and rewrite line 1143 to preserve the same reader autonomy.
2. Restore the baseline's stronger claim in the Reader's Part and retain the Final Note's direct conscription.
3. Write a deliberate middle position that invites recognition without asserting the reader's inner state.

Until that choice is made, the closing cannot receive semantic approval.

## Promise and consistency audit

### Measurability promise

Line 127 says later chapters will work to make coherence measurable across nervous systems, institutions, ecologies, and the relationship between intelligence and life. Later chapters name possible indicators and examples, but they do not provide an operational definition or method across all four levels. The manuscript should either fulfill the promise with a bounded measurement framework or change the sentence to promise an examination of what measurement would require.

### Trust-architecture promise

Line 397 promises a later chapter specifically examining the trust architecture and the choices meant to prevent reputation gaming, surveillance, and digital caste. Line 423 promises detailed failure modes and specific conditions under which Providence would choose failure rather than capture. Chapter Seven examines risks in physiological measurement, and Chapter Nine discusses governance commitments, but neither supplies the promised trust mechanism or a specific refusal protocol. The manuscript should add that material, move the promise to the later volume that contains it, or narrow the promise.

### Collaborator-access promise

Line 1149 says there are ways for a prospective builder to learn more, but the manuscript contains no link, contact path, repository, or next action. A standalone manuscript or PDF therefore makes an invitation it cannot satisfy. The publication should provide a durable route or remove the claim.
