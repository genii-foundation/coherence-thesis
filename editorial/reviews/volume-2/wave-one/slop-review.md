## Conclusion

**Result: FAIL pending editorial debt paydown and author decisions.**

The revision is materially leaner and more legible than the baseline. Its best passages have direct argument, vivid examples, and a recognizable prophetic register. It is not yet publish-ready under the new standard. The remaining burden is not a handful of banned phrases. It is structural. False antithesis, symmetrical lists, repeated chapter scaffolds, conceptual laundering, empirical overstatement, generic uplift, and reader conscription still recur often enough to sound generated. Several passages use exact numbers or implementation language without supplying a verification path. Several chapter conclusions repeat the same ascent toward urgency, builders, fragments, and the next civilization.

The detector reports 666 advisory warnings after this pass. That number is not a defect count. Many findings are legitimate taxonomies, deliberate anaphora, technical terms, or necessary wayfinding. It does, however, accurately reveal the manuscript's remaining concentration of recurring rhetorical machinery. A developmental consolidation pass, a primary-source evidence pass, and an author-guided decision on the prophetic register are required before a slop review can pass.

## Intentional exceptions retained

1. **Ampersand in display matter.** `Bio-Consensus & the Currency of Presence` at line 457 retains the ampersand. It gives the heading pace and visual balance. Expanding it mechanically would make the heading heavier and would violate the repository's own contextual ampersand rule.
2. **Ordinary compound hyphens.** Terms such as `nervous-system`, `civilization-building`, `open-source`, and `founder-as-permanent-authority` remain because they are grammatical compounds, not stylized punctuation.
3. **Title-page and section display units.** Short isolated lines at 1 to 25, part dividers, chapter labels, epigraph attributions, and colophon matter remain display language. The repeated title block at 1 to 7 still needs a structural decision and is handed off below.
4. **Ritual punctuation and refrains.** The `. : .` ornaments at 47, 57, and 1191, the blessing at 1135, and `The work belongs to whoever does it` at 521 and 1193 were retained. They are voice-bearing display units, although the repeated refrain still belongs in the developmental consolidation audit.
5. **Necessary lists.** The five-layer architecture, four unsolved problems, named intellectual lineages, and audience-specific closing sections require parallel structure. Their presence is not itself synthetic. The review flags only excess symmetry or unsupported completeness inside them.
6. **Implementation uncertainty.** Repeated words such as `intended`, `proposed`, `research question`, and `not the current MVP` remain because they preserve the difference between present fact and future design.
